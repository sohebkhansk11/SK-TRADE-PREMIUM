# CONCEPT #12 — Supportive Indicators & Oscillators
## Complete Detailed Understanding + 12 Highly Advanced AI Extensions

---

## PART 1 — CURRENT STATE IN EA

Indicators exist in the EA currently as visual display overlays and signal triggers only. Bollinger Bands and ATR are referenced in the weight matrix for zone sizing (ATR-based cluster merge radius) and for DD calculations, but NONE of the indicator price levels themselves are added to the S/R pool as standalone weighted levels. VWAP is not implemented. Volume Profile is not implemented. Ichimoku is not implemented. Parabolic SAR and Supertrend are not implemented. Oscillator readings (RSI, Stochastic, MACD, CCI, Williams %R, ADX, MFI) are referenced in signal logic only — not as weight modifiers on S/R levels.

Extension goal: (1) Add every dynamic indicator price level to the S/R pool as a weighted level object — Bollinger Bands, Keltner, Donchian, ATR Envelopes, VWAP family, Volume Profile, Ichimoku lines, SAR, Supertrend. (2) Add all 7 pure oscillators as attribute/contextual engines that modify the weight of nearby S/R levels when they reach extreme readings or produce divergence. (3) Unify all of this under the same state-tracking and grade-assignment framework used by every other concept.

---

## PART 2 — ARCHITECTURE OVERVIEW

This concept splits into two distinct layers:

**Layer 1 — Price-Level Indicators (count-contributing):** These indicators produce a specific price at any given moment. That price is added to the S/R pool as a weighted level object, tracked across its lifecycle (Active → Testing → Breached → Historical). They have distinct geometry — an exact price coordinate. 55 types across 4 categories.

**Layer 2 — Pure Oscillators (all attribute/contextual):** RSI, Stochastic, MACD, CCI, Williams %R, ADX, MFI. These produce a VALUE (e.g., RSI = 73) rather than a PRICE. They have no price coordinate to add to the S/R pool. They instead act as multipliers and weight modifiers on NEARBY S/R levels — when the oscillator reaches an extreme, it elevates the significance of the S/R level price is currently approaching. 7 oscillators, all attribute extensions.

### ═══ CATEGORY 1 — Volatility Band Indicators (15 types) ═══

These indicators create upper and lower price boundaries that expand during volatility and contract during quiet periods. They function as dynamic S/R — as price approaches them, probability of reversal is elevated. As price breaks through them, they flip to reversed S/R.

---

#### ── BOLLINGER BANDS (5 types) ──

**Type 1 — BB Upper (20-period, 2σ standard)**

Geometry: `BB_Upper = SMA(20) + 2 × StdDev(20)`. The 2σ upper band. At any given bar, this line is positioned 2 standard deviations above the 20-period simple moving average. Statistically, approximately 95% of all price action falls INSIDE the 2σ bands. When price touches or closes at the 2σ upper band, it has reached a statistically extreme reading — only 2.5% of historical closes land at or above this level.
Signal as S/R: Strong dynamic resistance. Price touching the BB Upper = the current price is at the 95th percentile of recent price distribution. In a ranging market, the BB Upper is the highest-probability short S/R. In a trending market, repeated touches of BB Upper without closing above it = trend running out of steam.
Key behavior: The BB Upper MOVES with price — it is not a fixed line. A sustained breakout ABOVE the BB Upper (multiple closes above) = a breakout event — the move is so strong it has exceeded 95% statistical probability. In this case the BB Upper shifts to support for the continuation.
S/R level generated: The exact price of the BB Upper at the current bar. Updated each bar.

---

**Type 2 — BB Mid (20-period SMA)**

Geometry: `BB_Mid = SMA(20)`. The central band — the 20-period SMA. This is the mean-reversion center of the Bollinger Band system. It exists simultaneously in the EMA/SMA Dynamic S/R concept (as SMA20) but is tracked SEPARATELY here as a Bollinger Band structural component because its S/R context is different: as BB Mid, it is the midpoint between upper and lower bands — the gravitational center of the band system.
Signal as S/R: Mean-reversion target level. When price has touched the BB Upper and begins to fade, the BB Mid = the first natural destination. When price has touched the BB Lower and begins to recover, the BB Mid = the first target. In trending markets, price tends to "walk" along one band and only return to the BB Mid at the end of the leg. The BB Mid = the equilibrium price for the most recent 20 bars.
Cross-concept note: As SMA20, this level already exists in the weight matrix via Concept #2 (EMA/SMA Dynamic S/R). When the BB Mid and SMA20 coincide at the same price (which they always do — they ARE the same calculation), this represents a same-source dual-context level. Weight from both sources stacks (Concept 2 weight + Concept 12 weight contributed).

---

**Type 3 — BB Lower (20-period, 2σ standard)**

Mirror of BB Upper. `BB_Lower = SMA(20) − 2 × StdDev(20)`. Dynamic support at the 5th percentile of the 20-bar price distribution.
Signal as S/R: Strong dynamic support. Price at the BB Lower = oversold relative to 20-period distribution. In ranging markets = highest-probability long S/R. In downtrends, price walking along the BB Lower = trend continuation. Sustained closes below BB Lower = breakdown.
S/R level generated: BB Lower price at the current bar.

---

**Type 4 — BB Upper Wide (20-period, 3σ extended)**

Geometry: `BB_Upper_Wide = SMA(20) + 3 × StdDev(20)`. The 3σ outer band. Statistically, only 0.15% of bars close at or above the 3σ upper band — this is an extreme outlier event. The 3σ band exists outside the standard 2σ band and marks the territory of extreme volatility events: flash crashes (recovered), earnings gaps, news spikes, stop-hunt raids.
Signal as S/R: Extreme resistance / capitulation zone. When price reaches the 3σ BB Upper, the probability of a sharp reversal is the highest of any indicator level in the system. Institutional algorithms often have hard-coded sell programs at 3σ extremes. Any time price trades at or above the 3σ BB Upper = the move is in the top 0.15% of historical distribution — exhaustion is imminent.
S/R level generated: 3σ upper band price. This level is typically much further from current price than the 2σ band — only becomes relevant during high-volatility conditions.

---

**Type 5 — BB Lower Wide (20-period, 3σ extended)**

Mirror of Type 4. `BB_Lower_Wide = SMA(20) − 3 × StdDev(20)`. Extreme support / capitulation low. Price at or below 3σ lower band = extreme oversold — the bottom 0.15% of historical distribution. Maximum panic / stop-hunt zone. Reversal probability at 3σ lower band is extremely high in all market conditions except true trending breakdowns (in which case a sustained close below 3σ = genuine catastrophic sell event).
S/R level generated: 3σ lower band price.

---

#### ── KELTNER CHANNELS (3 types) ──

**Type 6 — KC Upper (20-period EMA, 2.0×ATR)**

Geometry: `KC_Upper = EMA(20) + 2 × ATR(10)`. Unlike Bollinger Bands which use standard deviation (absolute distance), Keltner Channels use ATR (Average True Range) for their width. This makes Keltner Channels insensitive to sudden price spikes (which would inflate standard deviation and widen BB dramatically) but responsive to actual volatility over 10 bars.
Signal as S/R: Dynamic resistance based on ATR expansion. When the KC Upper is used ALONGSIDE Bollinger Bands, the relationship between them is critical: if BB Upper BREAKS ABOVE KC Upper (BB expands wider than KC) = a BB Squeeze breakout is occurring (high-velocity move). If BB Upper is INSIDE KC = Squeeze compression phase (low volatility, coil building). The KC Upper level itself = ATR-normalized dynamic resistance.
Distinction from BB Upper: BB Upper widens on volatility (including single-candle spikes). KC Upper widens only on sustained ATR expansion. KC Upper is MORE STABLE than BB Upper during choppy conditions.

---

**Type 7 — KC Mid (20-period EMA)**

Geometry: `KC_Mid = EMA(20)`. The central EMA of the Keltner Channel system. This is EMA20 — same as in Concept #2 (EMA Dynamic S/R). Tracked separately here as KC Mid because its role within the Keltner system is identical to BB Mid's role in the Bollinger system: mean-reversion center and equilibrium. As KC Mid = EMA20, same dual-context weight stacking applies as with BB Mid = SMA20.

---

**Type 8 — KC Lower (20-period EMA, 2.0×ATR)**

Mirror of KC Upper. `KC_Lower = EMA(20) − 2 × ATR(10)`. Dynamic ATR-normalized support. The KC Lower is more stable than BB Lower during volatile conditions. Primary use: the KC Lower during quiet markets = the key S/R support where pullbacks in uptrends find buyers.

---

#### ── DONCHIAN CHANNELS (3 types) ──

**Type 9 — DC Upper (20-period highest high)**

Geometry: `DC_Upper = Highest High of last 20 bars`. The Donchian Channel is the simplest possible envelope — it's just the highest high and lowest low over N bars. The DC Upper = the exact price of the most recent 20-bar high. This is a structural level in the most literal sense — the highest point of the recent range.
Signal as S/R: DC Upper = the BSL cluster for the recent N bars. Every bar that has traded up to the DC Upper is a bull who bought at that high and is now holding near breakeven. A breakout above the DC Upper = all of those traders are now profitable — they become holders who will defend the level if price pulls back. A failure at the DC Upper = the current high is being respected as resistance.
Historical significance: Donchian Channels were the basis for the legendary Turtle Trading system (Richard Dennis). The 20-bar and 55-bar Donchian breakouts are among the oldest and most-watched levels in professional trend-following systems. Institutional trend-following funds that use systematic breakout strategies (there are many) typically trade PRECISELY at the DC Upper breakout — making it a self-fulfilling S/R.

---

**Type 10 — DC Midline (midpoint of DC Upper + DC Lower)**

Geometry: `DC_Mid = (DC_Upper + DC_Lower) / 2`. The precise midpoint of the N-bar range. This level = the equilibrium of the N-bar distribution — the price that divides the entire recent range in half.
Signal as S/R: DC Midline = the 50% retracement of the N-bar range. In a trending move, the DC Mid often acts as the key pullback target. In a ranging market, the DC Mid = the gravity center that price oscillates around. It is the Donchian equivalent of the BB Mid / KC Mid mean-reversion center.

---

**Type 11 — DC Lower (20-period lowest low)**

Mirror of DC Upper. `DC_Lower = Lowest Low of last 20 bars`. The exact price of the most recent 20-bar low. The SSL cluster for the recent N bars. All traders who bought anywhere in the 20-bar range have their worst-case stop below this level. DC Lower breakdown = all those stops trigger = cascade. DC Lower holding = the floor holds.

---

#### ── ATR ENVELOPE LEVELS (4 types) ──

**Type 12 — ATR Envelope +1 ATR (above)**

Geometry: `ATR_Env_+1 = Reference_Price + 1 × ATR(14)`. The reference price is typically the current session open, or a selected MA (often SMA20 or EMA21). The +1 ATR level marks the price that is exactly 1 Average True Range above the reference. ATR is the normalizer — it scales the level relative to the instrument's CURRENT volatility, not a fixed pip value.
Signal as S/R: +1 ATR above the session open or key reference = the "one ATR move" target. In normal market conditions, 1 ATR is approximately the expected range for the current trading session. Price reaching +1 ATR from the open = the session has consumed its normal expected range in the upward direction. S/R significance: professional traders often set profit targets at ±1 ATR from entry. The concentration of profit-taking at this level = price reaction.

---

**Type 13 — ATR Envelope +2 ATR (above)**

Geometry: `ATR_Env_+2 = Reference_Price + 2 × ATR(14)`. Two average true ranges above the reference. This represents a 2× expected-range move — twice the normal daily range. Price reaching +2 ATR in a single session = an unusually strong move. Institutional take-profit systems at major funds often use 2 ATR as a profit target for trend-following positions. The clustering of these profit takes = S/R reaction at the 2 ATR level.
Signal as S/R: The 2 ATR level = the extreme end of a normal trading session. If price is approaching 2 ATR from the session open, the day is already a strong trend day. Take-profit selling and mean-reversion buying both converge at this level from different directions = high-probability reaction zone.

---

**Type 14 — ATR Envelope -1 ATR (below)**

Mirror of Type 12 downward. `ATR_Env_-1 = Reference_Price − 1 × ATR(14)`. The session low expected range threshold. Price reaching -1 ATR from the open = normal expected range consumed downward. Mean-reversion buyers and short profit-taking sellers converge here.

---

**Type 15 — ATR Envelope -2 ATR (below)**

Mirror of Type 13 downward. `ATR_Env_-2 = Reference_Price − 2 × ATR(14)`. Two standard daily ranges below the reference. Extreme session low. Highest probability mean-reversion point within the session context. Institutional buy programs triggered by 2 ATR declines are well-documented in quantitative trading literature.

### ═══ CATEGORY 2 — VWAP Family (17 types) ═══

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the single most-used institutional reference in intraday trading. Every major institution, market maker, and algorithm tracks VWAP. A trade above the VWAP = paid more than the average weighted price of the day = buying at a premium (institutional cost). A trade below VWAP = bought at a discount. VWAP is the "fair value" price of the session from a volume-weighted perspective. Its standard deviation bands (σ bands) create high-probability S/R zones because institutional algorithms are programmed to buy at -1σ/-2σ and sell at +1σ/+2σ systematically.

**VWAP calculation:** `VWAP = Σ(Price × Volume) / Σ(Volume)` where Price = typical price (H+L+C)/3. This resets at the start of each anchor period.

**Standard deviation bands:** `SD = √[Σ(Volume × (Price − VWAP)²) / Σ(Volume)]`. The bands are: VWAP ± 1σ, ± 2σ, ± 3σ.

---

#### ── SESSION VWAP (7 types) ──

**Type 16 — Session VWAP (core)**

The VWAP reset at the start of each trading session (for forex: typically each New York / London open). This is the most-watched intraday level for every institutional participant — banks, hedge funds, market makers, and algo programs. Price above Session VWAP = the average buyer for the day is profitable → positive sentiment reinforces holding and buying on dips. Price below Session VWAP = the average buyer for the day is losing → selling pressure to cut losses reinforces.
S/R: Session VWAP = the most significant dynamic intraday S/R level in existence. Banks execute large orders trying to be as close to VWAP as possible (VWAP execution algorithms are the most commonly used execution method at major banks). Huge order volume is concentrated near the Session VWAP. Every test of VWAP from either side = massive order flow concentration.

---

**Type 17 — Session VWAP +1σ**

`Session VWAP + 1 × session standard deviation`. Approximately 68% of all volume-weighted price action in the session falls WITHIN the ±1σ bands. The +1σ level = the top of the "fair value" zone. When price reaches +1σ = it has moved 1 standard deviation above the session's volume-weighted mean. Institutional sell programs (designed to sell into strength above VWAP) are most active at the +1σ level. In a ranging session, +1σ = the systematic short S/R. In a trending bullish session, +1σ = a consolidation target that price bounces from to continue higher.

---

**Type 18 — Session VWAP +2σ**

`Session VWAP + 2 × session standard deviation`. Approximately 95% of all volume-weighted price action falls within ±2σ. The +2σ upper band = the extreme of normal session range. Price reaching +2σ from Session VWAP = the session has seen an extraordinarily large move relative to its own volume history. Institutional automated sell programs are heavily concentrated at +2σ — this is where VWAP-referenced execution algorithms place their furthest take-profit levels. +2σ = highest-probability session reversal or rest zone.

---

**Type 19 — Session VWAP +3σ**

`Session VWAP + 3 × session standard deviation`. Only 0.3% of volume-weighted session bars reach the ±3σ level. This is the extreme outlier zone — only happens during major news events, flash crashes, or extraordinary institutional activity. When price reaches +3σ from Session VWAP, it is by definition an abnormal event. Reversal from +3σ is among the most reliable intraday short signals in existence — the move has exceeded 99.7% of normal session behavior. Often coincides with news-driven spikes or stop-hunt raids above key levels.

---

**Type 20 — Session VWAP -1σ**

`Session VWAP − 1 × session standard deviation`. Mirror of Type 17. The bottom of the session's fair value zone. Institutional buy programs (buy weakness below VWAP) are most active at -1σ. In a ranging session = highest-probability long S/R from below VWAP. In a trending bearish session = consolidation bounce level that fails and price continues lower.

---

**Type 21 — Session VWAP -2σ**

Mirror of Type 18. Extreme lower band. Institutional buy-the-dip programs concentrated here. Price at -2σ below Session VWAP = the session has seen an extraordinary selloff — probability of mean-reversion bounce extremely high unless a true breakdown is underway.

---

**Type 22 — Session VWAP -3σ**

Mirror of Type 19. The most extreme session low signal. Price below -3σ of Session VWAP = flash crash / capitulation territory. Any price that reaches -3σ from VWAP and then recovers within the session = one of the most powerful bullish reversals possible. The -3σ low becomes a permanent historical reference level.

---

#### ── WEEKLY VWAP (5 types) ──

**Type 23 — Weekly VWAP**

VWAP anchored to the week open — resets at the start of each trading week (Monday open). The Weekly VWAP represents the volume-weighted average price for the entire week so far. It is the institutional swing-level equivalent of Session VWAP. Where Session VWAP = intraday fair value, Weekly VWAP = multi-day fair value. Professional swing traders and institutional desks executing multi-day position programs reference Weekly VWAP as their primary guide. Price above Weekly VWAP on Wednesday = the week's buyers so far are profitable = bullish weekly structure.

---

**Type 24 — Weekly VWAP +1σ**

One weekly standard deviation above the Weekly VWAP. The top of the weekly fair value range. Swing-level overbought zone. Price reaching Weekly VWAP +1σ = the week has moved 1 standard deviation above its volume-weighted center. Professional swing sellers use this level to scale out of long positions and initiate short positions.

---

**Type 25 — Weekly VWAP +2σ**

Two weekly standard deviations above Weekly VWAP. Extreme weekly overbought zone. Price here = the week has had an extraordinary up-move relative to its own volume history. A weekly close above Weekly VWAP +2σ = one of the strongest weekly bullish signals. A TOUCH of Weekly VWAP +2σ followed by rejection = highest-probability weekly reversal level.

---

**Type 26 — Weekly VWAP -1σ**

One weekly standard deviation below Weekly VWAP. Bottom of weekly fair value range. Swing-level oversold zone. Professional weekly buy programs concentrated here.

---

**Type 27 — Weekly VWAP -2σ**

Two weekly standard deviations below Weekly VWAP. Extreme weekly oversold zone. Institutional weekly accumulation level. Price reaching -2σ from Weekly VWAP = extraordinary weekly down-move.

---

#### ── MONTHLY VWAP (5 types) ──

**Type 28 — Monthly VWAP**

VWAP anchored to the month open — resets at the first bar of each calendar month. Monthly VWAP = the macro volume-weighted fair value for the month. This is one of the most significant institutional reference levels because options pricing, futures delivery, and monthly performance benchmarking all reference monthly price distribution. Funds tracking monthly P&L use Monthly VWAP as the true "cost of the month" reference. Price above Monthly VWAP by month-end = the month closed with most volume profitable = monthly positive structure. Price below Monthly VWAP = most monthly volume is underwater = bearish monthly structure.

---

**Type 29 — Monthly VWAP +1σ**

One monthly standard deviation above Monthly VWAP. Top of the monthly fair value zone. Monthly overbought threshold. Institutions that benchmarked at monthly VWAP and saw their positions reach +1σ = fully achieving their monthly return target. Systematic monthly profit-taking concentrated here.

---

**Type 30 — Monthly VWAP +2σ**

Extreme monthly upper band. Price reaching Monthly VWAP +2σ within a single month = one of the most extraordinary monthly moves in market history. Only major trend months reach +2σ from Monthly VWAP. When it happens, this level = the most significant S/R level of the month — the month's absolute extreme high-probability reversal zone.

---

**Type 31 — Monthly VWAP -1σ**

One monthly standard deviation below Monthly VWAP. Monthly fair-value lower boundary. Institutional monthly buy programs triggered here.

---

**Type 32 — Monthly VWAP -2σ**

Extreme monthly lower band. Monthly capitulation level. Only extraordinary monthly selloffs reach -2σ from Monthly VWAP. This level = the most significant monthly support when reached.

### ═══ CATEGORY 3 — Volume Profile (12 types) ═══

Volume Profile is the distribution of all volume traded at each specific price level over a defined period. It shows WHERE (at which price) the most trading activity occurred, not just when. The three key levels from any volume profile are the POC, VAH, and VAL.

**Core concepts:**
- **POC (Point of Control):** The single price level where the MOST volume was traded over the period. This is the price that the market spent the most time at and where the most transactions occurred. POC = the highest-conviction fair value price of the period — the price where buyers and sellers transacted more than at any other price.
- **VAH (Value Area High):** The upper price boundary of the value area — the zone containing 70% of all the period's volume. Everything above the VAH = premium — price paid above the typical trading range.
- **VAL (Value Area Low):** The lower price boundary of the value area — the lower boundary of the 70% volume zone. Everything below the VAL = discount — price below the typical trading range.
- **Value Area:** The range between VAH and VAL containing 70% of all traded volume. This is where the majority of institutional business was conducted for the period. Price returning to the value area = returning to where most money changed hands = strong magnetic attraction.

---

#### ── SESSION VOLUME PROFILE (3 types) ──

**Type 33 — Session POC**

The price level with the most volume traded in the current session (today). This is the most-transacted price of the day — the day's "fair value" consensus. The Session POC is extremely significant as S/R because: (1) The most volume = the most buy and sell orders transacted. (2) Every institution that bought at the POC is watching to see if price returns — they will defend their position price. (3) Every institution that sold at the POC will use any return to POC to add to their short. The Session POC = the focal point of every active position from the day's trading.
Session POC behavior: When price returns to the Session POC after moving away, it frequently stalls or reverses — the POC is where trapped longs and shorts from the session converge. A break through the Session POC after a return = a definitive directional signal.

---

**Type 34 — Session VAH**

The upper boundary of the 70% volume zone for the current session. Price above Session VAH = trading in the premium zone — only 15% of the session's volume occurred above this level. Sellers who were active in the premium zone are watching for price to return to the VAH — it becomes resistance on a return. Buy-the-dip traders who bought in the value area set targets AT the VAH (returning to fair value's top).

---

**Type 35 — Session VAL**

The lower boundary of the 70% volume zone for the current session. Price below Session VAL = trading in the discount zone. Buyers who accumulated in the value area are watching for price to fall to the VAL — it becomes support on a dip. Short sellers who sold premium set targets AT the VAL (returning to fair value's bottom). The VAL is the lower magnetic level — if price falls below VAL, next destination = Session POC then VAH on the other side (value area rule: once below VAL, price tends to traverse the entire value area to VAH).

---

#### ── DAILY VOLUME PROFILE (3 types) ──

**Type 36 — Daily POC**

Same as Session POC but anchored to the full calendar day (00:00–23:59 for 24h markets). In forex with overlapping sessions, the Daily POC represents the consensus transacted price across all sessions of the day — the global fair value for that calendar day. Previous Daily POC levels (prior day's POC) = significant historical support/resistance. Price frequently revisits the prior day's POC (DPOC) before making its next directional decision.

---

**Type 37 — Daily VAH**

Upper boundary of 70% volume for the full calendar day. The previous day's VAH is often used as a resistance reference (price that was premium the prior day = where sellers were active).

---

**Type 38 — Daily VAL**

Lower boundary of 70% volume for the full calendar day. The previous day's VAL = the discount zone floor. Strong support on any return below it from above.

---

#### ── WEEKLY VOLUME PROFILE (3 types) ──

**Type 39 — Weekly POC**

The price with the most volume traded over the entire week. The Weekly POC = the week's consensus transacted price — the price the week "wanted to be at" most. Weeks where price closes near the Weekly POC = indecisive week with balanced buying/selling. Weeks that close far from the Weekly POC = trend week. The Weekly POC becomes a key reference for the following week — price returns to last week's POC before making directional decisions.

---

**Type 40 — Weekly VAH**

Upper boundary of the week's 70% volume zone. The weekly premium zone ceiling. Previous weekly VAH = key resistance for the following week's trading.

---

**Type 41 — Weekly VAL**

Lower boundary of the week's 70% volume zone. The weekly discount zone floor. Previous weekly VAL = key support for the following week.

---

#### ── MONTHLY VOLUME PROFILE (3 types) ──

**Type 42 — Monthly POC**

The highest-volume price of the entire month. The Monthly POC = the month's macro fair value consensus — the price the market spent the most time at over 20–22 trading days. Monthly POC levels are extremely significant because they represent: (1) The average cost basis of the month's institutional participants. (2) Options dealers' hedging reference (maximum gamma exposure often clusters near monthly POC). (3) The price magnetic center for any month-end activity. Monthly POCs from major trend months become long-term S/R references that persist for months or years.

---

**Type 43 — Monthly VAH**

Upper boundary of the month's 70% volume zone. The month's premium zone ceiling. Price above the Monthly VAH = trading in the extreme top 15% of the month's price range — institutional sellers were sparse here. Monthly VAH = key resistance. Breakout above Monthly VAH = the entire month's premium zone is exceeded = strong bullish signal.

---

**Type 44 — Monthly VAL**

Lower boundary of the month's 70% volume zone. The month's discount zone floor. Price below Monthly VAL = trading in the extreme bottom 15% of the month's range. Monthly VAL = key support. Breakdown below Monthly VAL = the entire month's discount zone is exceeded = strong bearish signal.

#### ── ICHIMOKU KINKO HYO (7 types) ──

Ichimoku Kinko Hyo ("equilibrium chart at a glance") is a complete trend-following system that produces 5 distinct lines simultaneously — each one a separate S/R level with a different time horizon. Developed by Goichi Hosoda over 30 years of research, it is one of the most widely-used systems by Japanese institutional traders and increasingly adopted by global institutions. Every Ichimoku line = a distinct S/R level in the pool.

---

**Type 45 — Tenkan-sen (Conversion Line, 9-period)**

Geometry: `Tenkan = (Highest High of last 9 bars + Lowest Low of last 9 bars) / 2`. The 9-bar midpoint price. This is NOT a moving average — it is the geometric center of the last 9 bars' range. It moves only when a new 9-bar high or low is set.
Signal as S/R: The fastest Ichimoku line — the 9-bar equilibrium. In a strong trend, price stays above Tenkan (bullish) or below Tenkan (bearish). Tenkan is the first level of dynamic support in an uptrend — the closest trailing support line. Tenkan crosses above Kijun = bullish Tenkan/Kijun cross (TK Cross) = strong bullish signal. Tenkan as S/R: the 9-bar midpoint is where short-term traders set stops and reversals.

---

**Type 46 — Kijun-sen (Base Line, 26-period)**

Geometry: `Kijun = (Highest High of last 26 bars + Lowest Low of last 26 bars) / 2`. The 26-bar midpoint — a slower and more significant equilibrium. The Kijun = the market's intermediate-term fair value. It represents approximately 1 month of trading (26 sessions ≈ 1 trading month on D1).
Signal as S/R: The primary Ichimoku S/R line. In trending markets, the Kijun is the definitive "line in the sand" — price above Kijun = bullish. Price below = bearish. The Kijun is the standard Ichimoku support/resistance for swing trading. When price pulls back to Kijun in a bull trend = the highest-probability long entry in the Ichimoku system. The Kijun "flat zone" (where Kijun is horizontal) = the current market equilibrium and a major congestion reference.

---

**Type 47 — Senkou Span A (Leading Span A)**

Geometry: `SpanA = (Tenkan + Kijun) / 2`, plotted 26 bars INTO THE FUTURE. SpanA is the midpoint of Tenkan and Kijun, projected 26 bars forward. It forms one boundary of the Ichimoku Cloud (Kumo).
Signal as S/R: SpanA is a future S/R level — it shows where the Kumo boundary WILL BE in 26 bars if nothing changes. Because it changes with every Tenkan and Kijun update, it is a dynamic forward-projected S/R. The key use: when current price is approaching a future SpanA level (26 bars from the origin), that level is a major S/R destination. SpanA and SpanB together form the Cloud — and SpanA = the more reactive boundary (changes with Tenkan/Kijun shifts).

---

**Type 48 — Senkou Span B (Leading Span B)**

Geometry: `SpanB = (Highest High of last 52 bars + Lowest Low of last 52 bars) / 2`, plotted 26 bars INTO THE FUTURE. SpanB is the 52-bar midpoint projected forward. SpanB changes MUCH more slowly than SpanA — only when a new 52-bar high or low is set.
Signal as S/R: SpanB = the more stable and therefore more significant Kumo boundary. Where SpanA reacts quickly to changes, SpanB can remain flat for extended periods — creating a horizontal S/R reference. Flat SpanB = a major static S/R zone that lasts for 52+ bars. When price approaches a flat SpanB = approaching the 52-bar equilibrium center = a maximum-significance Ichimoku S/R encounter. SpanB is the "harder" Kumo boundary.

---

**Type 49 — Chikou Span (Lagging Span)**

Geometry: `Chikou = Current Close`, plotted 26 bars INTO THE PAST. The Chikou is unique — it places today's close on the chart 26 bars back in time. Its position relative to price bars 26 bars ago reveals whether the current price is in bullish or bearish territory historically.
Signal as S/R: When the Chikou (current close displaced to the past) coincides with a historical price cluster from 26 bars ago, that historical cluster BECOMES a present-day S/R reference. The Chikou creates S/R levels by identifying where current price intersects with historical price structure from exactly 26 bars prior. Chikou above the price bars behind it = bullish. Chikou below = bearish. When the Chikou itself touches a past high or low = an S/R interaction across time.

---

**Type 50 — Kumo Top (Cloud Top)**

Geometry: `Kumo_Top = MAX(SpanA, SpanB)`. The higher of the two Span lines at any given bar. The top of the Ichimoku Cloud.
Signal as S/R: The Kumo Top = the strongest Ichimoku resistance level. Price approaching the Kumo Top from below = approaching the cloud = the most significant Ichimoku S/R encounter. The Cloud "absorbs" price — the thicker the cloud, the more significant the S/R. Cloud Top resistance has the combined weight of both SpanA and SpanB — the hardest ceiling in the Ichimoku system for bearish-trend markets trying to recover. A breakout ABOVE the Cloud Top = a full Ichimoku bullish structure confirmation. The Kumo Top is tracked as a separate level from SpanA and SpanB because it always represents the absolute ceiling of the cloud at any bar.

---

**Type 51 — Kumo Bottom (Cloud Bottom)**

Geometry: `Kumo_Bottom = MIN(SpanA, SpanB)`. The lower of the two Span lines. The bottom of the Ichimoku Cloud.
Signal as S/R: The Kumo Bottom = the strongest Ichimoku support level. Price approaching the Kumo Bottom from above = approaching the cloud from the bullish side = the critical test of the cloud support. A hold of the Kumo Bottom = the bullish trend structure is intact. A break below Kumo Bottom = the cloud has been penetrated downward — not yet a full breakdown unless price also breaks below Kumo Top from the other side. The cloud creates a "zone of indecision" between Kumo Top and Kumo Bottom — price inside the cloud = no clear trend. The Kumo Bottom = the floor of that zone.

---

#### ── PARABOLIC SAR (2 types) ──

**Type 52 — SAR Bullish (dots below price = dynamic trailing support)**

Geometry: The Parabolic SAR (Stop and Reverse) formula: each bar's SAR value is calculated by an accelerating formula that "chases" price upward during a bullish run. The SAR starts at the recent swing low and accelerates toward price as the bull run continues. In bullish mode, SAR dots appear BELOW price, inching upward with each bar.
Signal as S/R: Bullish SAR dots = a trail of potential reversal levels below current price. Each SAR dot = the level at which the SAR system considers the bullish trend OVER if price closes below it. Institutional trailing stop programs often reference the Parabolic SAR level as their trailing stop placement. The SAR dot price = the convergence point of all SAR-following institutional stop levels. As price approaches a SAR level from above during a pullback = the largest concentration of buy stops (both protective and systematic entry) in the Parabolic SAR universe are clustered at that price.
Key S/R behavior: The current Bullish SAR dot level = the "do not close below this" line for all Parabolic SAR system traders. Price touching the SAR dot from above = maximum buy order convergence. Price closing below the SAR dot = System flips to Bearish SAR → all buyers from the bullish run are now stopped out = cascading sell pressure.

---

**Type 53 — SAR Bearish (dots above price = dynamic trailing resistance)**

Mirror of Type 52. In bearish mode, SAR dots appear ABOVE price, descending with each bar — chasing price downward. Each SAR dot above = the level at which all short positions using Parabolic SAR as their trailing stop are stopped out (the "cover and flip" level). As price rallies toward the Bearish SAR dot from below = maximum short-covering and stop-out convergence. Price touching the Bearish SAR dot = the most likely short-cover zone. Price closing above the Bearish SAR = system flips to Bullish → cascading buy pressure from all the covering shorts.

---

#### ── SUPERTREND (2 types) ──

**Type 54 — Supertrend Support (green band below price)**

Geometry: `Supertrend_Upper = (High + Low)/2 + Multiplier × ATR(Period)`. When price closes ABOVE the Supertrend band, the trend is bullish and the band appears below price as green — a dynamic trailing support. Standard settings: ATR(10) × 3.0. The Supertrend is specifically designed to stay as CLOSE to price as possible while filtering out noise — it uses ATR to adapt its distance dynamically.
Signal as S/R: Supertrend Support = a tightly-followed trailing support level used by an enormous number of retail and algorithmic traders (Supertrend is one of the most popular indicators globally by usage volume). Because of its massive adoption, price approaching the Supertrend Support = approach of a massive cluster of buy stop orders from all Supertrend-following systems. The self-fulfilling nature of widely-used indicators = Supertrend Support levels are real S/R because millions of participants act on them simultaneously.
Key S/R behavior: Each touch of Supertrend Support that holds = the bullish trend confirmed intact. First close below Supertrend Support = trend flip to bearish. The flip level (where green Supertrend turns red) = one of the most significant S/R events in the Supertrend system — all long positions based on the Supertrend are stopped. This flip price = the institutional stop-out level.

---

**Type 55 — Supertrend Resistance (red band above price)**

Mirror of Type 54. When bearish, the Supertrend band appears above price as red trailing resistance. Price approaching the Supertrend Resistance from below = approach of all short-cover stop levels for Supertrend short position holders. First close above the red band = bullish flip → cascading short covering. The flip price = the stop-out level for all Supertrend bears.

## PART 4 — GRADE DEFINITIONS

| Grade | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **Strong** | Price touches the indicator level within 0.25×ATR precision AND at least one of: (a) indicator simultaneously aligns with another S/R method (trendline, swing, pivot); (b) indicator is at a multi-month historical extreme (BB Width at 1-year low = squeeze extreme, VWAP σ at 6-month extreme); (c) volume at the interaction is >1.5× the 20-bar average, confirming institutional activity at that exact level. |

## PART 5 — STATE DEFINITIONS

| State | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **Active** | The indicator level is live for the current period. Price has not yet reached this level in the current period/session. Full weight — it is an untested potential S/R destination. |

## PART 7 — PURE OSCILLATORS LAYER (All Attribute/Contextual — No Price Level)

These 7 oscillators produce readings rather than price coordinates. They function exclusively as weight-modifiers on nearby S/R levels. Each is described below with its specific mechanism for modifying S/R weight:

---

**OSC-1 — RSI (Relative Strength Index, 14-period)**

Formula: `RSI = 100 − (100 / (1 + RS))` where RS = Average Gain / Average Loss over 14 bars.

Weight modification mechanism: RSI modifies the weight of the nearest S/R level in the direction price is approaching it.
- RSI > 70 (overbought): Any resistance S/R level that price is approaching while RSI > 70 → weight of that resistance INCREASED by +0.20 (elevated conviction for the resistance holding). RSI > 80 → +0.35 (extreme overbought = very high rejection probability). RSI > 90 → +0.50 (extreme rarity = maximum rejection probability).
- RSI < 30 (oversold): Any support S/R level price is approaching while RSI < 30 → weight of that support INCREASED by +0.20. RSI < 20 → +0.35. RSI < 10 → +0.50.
- RSI 45–55 (neutral zone): No modification — midzone RSI = no directional conviction.
- RSI 50 line cross (bullish): Adds +0.10 to nearby resistance (breakout context). RSI 50 cross bearish: adds +0.10 to nearby support (breakdown context).

**RSI Divergence (attribute sub-type):**
- Regular Bullish Divergence: Price makes a lower low, RSI makes a higher low. The support S/R at the lower price low → weight INCREASED by +0.40 (divergence = institutional buying absorption at this level).
- Regular Bearish Divergence: Price makes a higher high, RSI makes a lower high. The resistance S/R at the higher price high → weight INCREASED by +0.40.
- Hidden Bullish Divergence: Price makes a higher low, RSI makes a lower low. The support where price made a higher low → +0.25 (continuation signal).
- Hidden Bearish Divergence: Price makes a lower high, RSI makes a higher high. The resistance → +0.25.

---

**OSC-2 — Stochastic Oscillator (14-period %K, 3-period %D)**

Formula: `%K = 100 × (Close − Lowest Low N) / (Highest High N − Lowest Low N)`. `%D = 3-period SMA of %K`.

Weight modification mechanism:
- %K > 80 or %K < 20 = overbought/oversold. Same directional weight boost as RSI: +0.15 at 80/20, +0.30 at 85/15, +0.45 at 90/10 extremes.
- Stochastic %K/%D Bullish Cross while below 20: The support S/R level at the cross price → +0.30 (stochastic cross from oversold = institutional bottom signal).
- Stochastic %K/%D Bearish Cross while above 80: The resistance S/R level at the cross price → +0.30.
- Stochastic Divergence (Bull/Bear Regular/Hidden): Same ±0.25 to ±0.40 modifiers as RSI divergence.

---

**OSC-3 — MACD (12/26/9 standard settings)**

Formula: `MACD Line = EMA(12) − EMA(26)`. `Signal Line = EMA(9) of MACD`. `Histogram = MACD − Signal`.

Weight modification mechanism:
- MACD Histogram sign (positive = bullish momentum, negative = bearish): Weight modification to S/R levels in the direction of the histogram: +0.10 to any S/R level that aligns with the histogram direction.
- MACD Bullish Cross (MACD crosses above Signal): Support S/R levels in the current vicinity → +0.20 (momentum turning bullish = support holding probability increased).
- MACD Bearish Cross: Resistance S/R → +0.20.
- MACD Zero Line Cross Bullish (MACD crosses above 0): Support near current price → +0.25 (trend confirmation = macro bullish).
- MACD Zero Line Cross Bearish: Resistance → +0.25.
- MACD Divergence (Regular Bull/Bear, Hidden Bull/Bear): Same +0.25 to +0.40 modifiers as RSI.
- MACD Histogram decreasing peak (taller bar then shorter bar = momentum weakening): Any resistance above this price → +0.15 (the move toward resistance is losing power).

---

**OSC-4 — CCI (Commodity Channel Index, 20-period)**

Formula: `CCI = (Typical Price − SMA(20 typical)) / (0.015 × Mean Deviation)`.

Weight modification mechanism:
- CCI > +100: Overbought. Resistance S/R above → +0.15. CCI > +200 → +0.30. CCI > +300 → +0.45.
- CCI < -100: Oversold. Support S/R below → +0.15. CCI < -200 → +0.30.
- CCI Zero Cross Bullish: Support → +0.15. Zero Cross Bearish: Resistance → +0.15.
- CCI Divergence: ±0.20 modifier matching the divergence type/direction.

---

**OSC-5 — Williams %R (14-period)**

Formula: `Williams %R = (Highest High N − Close) / (Highest High N − Lowest Low N) × -100`. Scale: 0 to -100. Readings near 0 = overbought. Readings near -100 = oversold. Thresholds: -20 = overbought, -80 = oversold.

Weight modification mechanism:
- Williams %R > -20 (overbought): Resistance above current price → +0.15. %R > -5 (extreme) → +0.30.
- Williams %R < -80 (oversold): Support below → +0.15. %R < -95 (extreme) → +0.30.
- Williams %R is faster-reacting than RSI — it can give earlier overbought/oversold signals. The combination of Williams %R extreme + RSI extreme at the same S/R level → stacked weight bonus.

---

**OSC-6 — ADX (Average Directional Index, 14-period)**

Formula: ADX uses the Directional Movement system: DM+ (upward movement component) and DM- (downward movement component) smoothed over 14 bars. ADX itself = the 14-period smoothed ratio of absolute DM difference to DM sum. ADX is NON-DIRECTIONAL — it measures only the STRENGTH of the trend, not its direction.

Weight modification mechanism (unique — ADX modifies ALL nearby S/R levels differently from momentum oscillators):
- ADX < 20 (ranging/weak trend): All S/R levels in the vicinity → weight INCREASED by +0.15 (in a ranging market, S/R levels are MORE likely to hold — price is bouncing between levels rather than breaking through them). Reversal probability at any S/R elevated.
- ADX 20–35 (developing trend): S/R levels in the TREND direction → weight DECREASED by -0.10 (trending market = levels against the trend are more likely to be broken). S/R levels WITH the trend → no change.
- ADX > 35 (strong trend): S/R levels AGAINST the trend → weight DECREASED by -0.20 (strong trend = counter-trend S/R reliability falls). S/R levels WITH the trend (pullback supports in uptrend, pullback resistances in downtrend) → INCREASED by +0.15 (the trend is strong = dips to support will find buyers).
- ADX > 50 (extreme trend): Counter-trend S/R → -0.35 (a level against an ADX 50+ trend is very likely to be blown through). With-trend S/R → +0.25.

ADX is the only oscillator that can DECREASE S/R level weights — because in a strong trend, many S/R levels that would normally hold are swept through.

---

**OSC-7 — MFI (Money Flow Index, 14-period)**

Formula: `MFI = 100 − (100 / (1 + Money Ratio))` where Money Ratio = Positive Money Flow / Negative Money Flow over 14 bars. Positive Money Flow = volume on bars where Typical Price was higher than prior bar. MFI = a volume-weighted RSI. It measures not just price momentum but whether the momentum is backed by volume.

Weight modification mechanism (identical in structure to RSI but more significant due to volume weighting):
- MFI > 80: Overbought with volume confirmation. Resistance above → +0.25 (stronger than RSI signal because it confirms volume participation). MFI > 90 → +0.40. MFI > 95 → +0.55.
- MFI < 20: Oversold with volume confirmation. Support below → +0.25. MFI < 10 → +0.40.
- MFI Divergence (Bull/Bear Regular): ±0.45 (stronger than RSI divergence because MFI divergence = price diverging from volume = very high conviction for S/R significance).
- MFI Divergence (Hidden Bull/Bear): ±0.30.

**Why MFI > RSI in weight terms:** RSI reflects price changes only — a large price move with zero volume still moves RSI. MFI requires volume participation. A price extreme with low volume (RSI extreme but MFI neutral) = potentially a thin-market spike. A price extreme with high volume (both RSI and MFI extreme simultaneously) = maximum institutional conviction → maximum S/R weight boost when they converge at the same level.

### ═══ EXTENSION 4: State Lifecycle Tracking (Active / Testing / Breached / Historical ×4) ═══

**What it is (Count-Contributing):** Four-state tracking system adapted specifically for dynamic indicator levels.

**Active state:** The indicator level exists for the current period and price has not yet engaged it. For dynamic indicators (BB, SAR, Supertrend, Ichimoku), the Active level is the current bar's calculated value. For VWAP, it is the current session's VWAP and deviation bands — all are Active until price reaches them. For Volume Profile, the Active POC/VAH/VAL are for the current ongoing session.
Weight: 1.0× base weight.

**Testing state:** Price is within 0.5×ATR of the indicator level. This is the engagement phase — the S/R level is being actively approached or contested right now.
Weight: 1.15× (elevated — the level is at its critical moment of validation or rejection).
Implementation: `IndicatorLevel.state = STATE_TESTING` when `MathAbs(current_price − level_price) < 0.5 × ATR_14`.

**Breached state:** Price has produced a FULL BAR CLOSE beyond the indicator level. For dynamic levels (BB, KC, SAR, Supertrend), a breach has specific meaning: a close above BB Upper = the move has exceeded 95th percentile distribution = breakout. Breach does NOT mean the level is deleted — it now acts as reversed S/R at the breach price.
For BB specifically: closing above BB Upper without subsequent reversion = BB Walk (price walking along the upper band in a strong trend). The BB Upper during a BB Walk = support on micro-pullbacks rather than resistance. `BB_Upper.direction = SUPPORT` during confirmed Walk state.
For SAR/Supertrend: Breach = FLIP. A breach of the SAR level = the system has flipped from Bullish to Bearish (or vice versa). The flip price = the most significant single-bar S/R event in the SAR/Supertrend system.
Weight after breach: Maintained at 0.90× (the level remains significant as reversed S/R).

**Historical state:** The period has ended (session closed, week ended, etc.) or for dynamic indicators: the bar when the indicator was at a key extreme is now more than N bars in the past. Historical VWAP, POC, VAH, VAL from prior sessions/weeks/months are ALL stored as Historical levels. The most significant historical levels (prior session VWAP, prior day POC) remain at 0.80× weight. Older historical levels decay further.
Historical retention policy: Session VWAP → kept for 5 sessions. Weekly VWAP → kept for 4 weeks. Monthly VWAP → kept for 3 months. Volume Profile POC → kept for 5 equivalent periods. Dynamic indicator extremes → kept for 20 bars.

### ═══ EXTENSION 5: Oscillator Confluence at S/R (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** When multiple oscillators simultaneously reach extreme readings at the moment price approaches a specific S/R level, the weight of that level is elevated proportionally to the number of converging oscillator signals.

**Single oscillator extreme:**
- 1 oscillator at extreme while price approaches S/R: +0.15 weight
- 1 oscillator at extreme + divergence: +0.35 weight

**Multi-oscillator convergence (the "oscillator stack"):**
- 2 oscillators simultaneously at extremes (RSI > 70 + Stochastic > 80): +0.30
- 3 oscillators at extremes (RSI + Stochastic + MFI): +0.50
- 4+ oscillators at extremes: +0.70 (maximum oscillator confluence bonus)

**Divergence stacking:**
- Single divergence type (RSI regular bull divergence): +0.35
- Two oscillators with same divergence type (RSI + MACD both show regular bull divergence at same price): +0.55
- Three oscillators all showing divergence: +0.75

**The "perfect storm" combination:**
All four of the following simultaneously:
(1) Price at a strong-grade S/R level (BB ±2σ or VWAP ±2σ)
(2) 3+ oscillators at extremes
(3) At least 2 oscillators showing divergence
(4) ADX < 20 (ranging market = highest reversal probability)
→ Total oscillator bonus: +0.90 (one of the highest single-concept weight modifiers in the system)

**Implementation:** `_CalcOscillatorBonus(level_price)`:
```
bonus = 0
osc_extremes = 0
osc_divergences = 0
For each of 7 oscillators:
   if (oscillator_at_extreme): osc_extremes++
   if (oscillator_divergence_at_level_price): osc_divergences++
bonus += WM_OSC_EXTREME_BONUS[osc_extremes]  // {0,0.15,0.30,0.50,0.60,0.70,0.70,0.70}

if (ADX < 20): bonus += 0.10  // ranging market amplifier
return min(bonus, 0.90)  // cap at 0.90
```

### ═══ EXTENSION 6: Bollinger Band Squeeze/Expansion State (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** The WIDTH of the Bollinger Bands (BB Upper − BB Lower, normalized by BB Mid) is itself a critical context indicator that modifies the weight and interpretation of all BB levels AND all nearby S/R levels.

**Band Width calculation:** `BandWidth = (BB_Upper − BB_Lower) / BB_Mid`. This normalizes the band width as a percentage of price, making it comparable across instruments and time.

**Squeeze state (BandWidth at N-bar low):**
Bollinger Squeeze = BB Width at its lowest reading in a specified lookback period. The squeeze represents historically low volatility — price coiling for an explosive move.

When in Squeeze state:

### ═══ EXTENSION 8: Volume Profile HVN/LVN Overlay (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** Beyond the primary POC/VAH/VAL levels (which are count-contributing types), the full volume profile histogram reveals additional High Volume Nodes (HVN) and Low Volume Nodes (LVN) that serve as secondary S/R within the value area.

**HVN (High Volume Node):**
A price level or small price zone within the profile where volume is significantly higher than surrounding prices — a secondary peak in the volume histogram (NOT the global POC, but a local maximum). HVNs represent secondary consensus prices — price transacted disproportionately heavily here, meaning many participants have cost basis at this level.
HVN as S/R: Price returning to an HVN = approaching a zone where many participants are breakeven or slightly profitable → they provide active limit orders at this price (buy at breakeven from the upside = support; sell at breakeven from the downside = resistance). HVN = areas of strong S/R because of the concentration of participants at that level.
Weight: HVN weight = POC weight × (HVN volume / POC volume). A secondary node at 80% of POC volume = weight at 80% of the POC S/R level weight.

**LVN (Low Volume Node):**
A price level or zone in the profile where volume is significantly LOWER than surrounding prices — a gap or thin area in the histogram. LVNs are the opposite of HVN: few participants transacted here = little resting order density = price moves through LVN zones RAPIDLY.
LVN as S/R: An LVN is NOT a traditional S/R level where price stalls — it is instead a VELOCITY ZONE. When price enters an LVN, it tends to move very quickly through it (no resting orders to slow it down) until it reaches the next HVN or POC on the other side. LVN positions mark where price will "jump" during a move.
Implementation: Flag the LVN zones in the profile. When the weight matrix's price proximity filter detects price approaching an LVN → flag all S/R levels on the FAR SIDE of the LVN as "next target" with elevated urgency (price will traverse the LVN quickly and reach those S/R levels faster than normal).

**HVN/LVN detection algorithm:**
```
histogram[price_bucket] = total volume at each 5-pip bucket
peak_volume = max(histogram)
HVN_threshold = peak_volume × 0.60  // >60% of peak = HVN
LVN_threshold = average_volume × 0.40  // <40% of average = LVN
For each bucket:
   if (histogram[bucket] > HVN_threshold): add HVN level
   if (histogram[bucket] < LVN_threshold AND bucket inside value area): flag LVN zone
```

**What it is:** A systematic divergence detection engine that identifies ALL 4 divergence types across ALL 7 oscillators simultaneously, at every S/R level in the pool.

**4 Divergence Types:**

**Regular Bullish Divergence:**
Price makes a lower low (LL). The oscillator makes a HIGHER low (HL). This means price went lower but momentum did NOT go lower — momentum is stronger than price indicates. The support S/R at the PRICE'S lower low = the location of the maximum divergence → weight elevated.
Signal: Institutional accumulation — buyers are absorbing the lower low without the oscillator confirming the new weakness. Price will typically reverse from this support.

**Regular Bearish Divergence:**
Price makes a higher high (HH). Oscillator makes a LOWER high (LH). Momentum weaker than price at the resistance. Signal: Distribution — sellers becoming active at the high without full momentum support.

**Hidden Bullish Divergence:**
Price makes a HIGHER low (HL) — a pullback in an uptrend. Oscillator makes a LOWER low (LL). Price didn't go as low as oscillator suggests — the trend is stronger than it appears. Signal: Continuation of the uptrend from the higher low support.

**Hidden Bearish Divergence:**
Price makes a LOWER high. Oscillator makes a HIGHER high. The downtrend is resuming — the rally was weaker than it appeared. Signal: Continuation of the downtrend from the lower high resistance.

**Cross-oscillator divergence scoring:**

### ═══ EXTENSION 11: Mean-Reversion Probability Score (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** A calculated score that quantifies how far price is from its mean indicators (BB Mid, VWAP, KC Mid) in ATR-normalized terms, and uses this distance to modify the weights of all S/R levels in the current vicinity.

**Distance metric:**
```
BB_Distance = (Current_Price - BB_Mid) / ATR(14)
VWAP_Distance = (Current_Price - Session_VWAP) / ATR(14)
KC_Distance = (Current_Price - KC_Mid) / ATR(14)
Average_Distance = (BB_Distance + VWAP_Distance + KC_Distance) / 3
```

**ATR-normalized distance thresholds:**
- Distance = 0 to ±0.5 ATR from mean: Near-mean zone. S/R levels near price have NORMAL weight (no modification — price is near equilibrium, direction is unclear, levels are tested on their own merit).
- Distance = ±0.5 to ±1.0 ATR from mean: Moderate extension. S/R levels on the SAME SIDE as the extension (e.g., resistance when price is above mean) → +0.10 (the extension increases probability that S/R on that side will hold for mean-reversion).
- Distance = ±1.0 to ±1.5 ATR from mean: Significant extension. S/R levels in the extension direction → +0.20. The BB Mid / VWAP / KC Mid themselves → flagged as "mean-reversion target" levels and their weights elevated by +0.15.
- Distance = ±1.5 to ±2.0 ATR from mean: Strong extension. +0.30 on extension-side S/R. Mean-level targets → +0.25 (strong pull of mean reversion at this distance).
- Distance > ±2.0 ATR from mean: Extreme extension. +0.45 on extension-side S/R (these S/R levels at extreme extension have very high reversal probability). Mean targets → +0.35 (price will almost certainly return to mean from this distance in the current period).

**Directional asymmetry adjustment:**
In a confirmed uptrend (price above all VWAPs, Ichimoku fully bullish, ADX > 25 with +DI > -DI): The mean-reversion probability score is HALVED for resistance levels (a trending market tends to run through S/R rather than mean-revert from it). Support levels during the trend → score is ENHANCED by ×1.25 (dips to mean are buying opportunities in a trend, not reversals — so the mean S/R has elevated weight as a re-entry point).

- Thick Cloud (Kumo_Thickness > 1.5×ATR): The cloud is a strong S/R zone. All S/R levels INSIDE the cloud receive a massive weight modifier → cloud levels get +0.40 (the cloud's thickness represents historical price indecision — many participants have cost basis inside the cloud = maximum order concentration). Price moving through a thick cloud will face strong friction.
- Normal Cloud (0.5–1.5×ATR): Standard weight for cloud levels.
- Thin Cloud (< 0.5×ATR): Weak S/R zone. Cloud levels → -0.15 weight (thin cloud = insufficient price history at this level = the cloud will not act as strong S/R — price will move through quickly).
- Flat Senkou Span B (not changing for 10+ bars): SpanB becomes a FIXED horizontal S/R. Flat SpanB weight → elevated by +0.20 (a flat 52-bar midpoint line = a hard, static S/R reference that price has remained stable around for 52 bars — equivalent to a 52-bar equilibrium level).

**Cloud Color (Bullish/Bearish Cloud):**
- Bullish Cloud (SpanA > SpanB — green cloud): All support S/R levels within the cloud → +0.15 (green cloud = structurally bullish zone — buyers more prevalent inside the cloud when it's green). All resistance levels within → +0.0 (no change).
- Bearish Cloud (SpanA < SpanB — red cloud): All resistance S/R levels within → +0.15. Support levels within → +0.0.
- Cloud Color Twist (Imminent flip from bullish to bearish or vice versa within the next 5 projected bars): ALL cloud S/R levels near the twist point → +0.25 (cloud twist = a major structural shift — the area where it twists is a pivotal S/R zone as the market transitions between regimes).

**Price Position Relative to Cloud:**

The Kumo Top below price = the key "cloud support" — if price pullback reaches Kumo Top → elevated weight +0.20 (Kumo support in a bullish structure is one of the most significant swing buy signals in Ichimoku).
- Price INSIDE Cloud: Indecision zone. ALL S/R levels within the cloud receive weight NEUTRALIZATION → weight modifier = 0.0 (inside the cloud = no directional bias from Ichimoku = S/R levels on their own merit only). Price moving from one side of the cloud to the other WITHIN the cloud = neutral drift.
- Price BELOW Cloud: Bearish macro structure. Mirror of above-cloud. Kumo Bottom above price = the "cloud resistance" — elevated by +0.20 when approached from below.
