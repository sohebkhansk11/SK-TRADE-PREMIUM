### ═══ CATEGORY 1 — Classic Reversal Patterns (24 types) ═══

#### BULLISH REVERSAL (12 patterns)

---

**1 — Inverse Head and Shoulders (IH&S)**

Geometry: Three-trough formation. Left Shoulder (first low) → Head (deepest low — the lowest point of the pattern) → Right Shoulder (third low, approximately equal height to Left Shoulder and HIGHER than the Head). A NECKLINE is drawn connecting the peaks between the troughs (the rally highs between Left Shoulder/Head and Head/Right Shoulder). When price body-closes ABOVE the neckline = pattern confirmed.
Signal: Strongest bullish reversal pattern. The Head = the final attempt by sellers to drive lower — they made a new low, but buyers stepped in so aggressively that subsequent selling could only reach the shoulder level. The neckline break = sellers have been definitively overcome. The IH&S is the standard pattern for institutional accumulation completion.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Neckline = primary S/R. Before breakout = resistance. After breakout = support on retest (the classic "retest the neckline" trade). This is the most traded level of the pattern.
(2) Pattern measured move target = Neckline + (Neckline − Head Low). This target level enters the pool as a future resistance (where the pattern's projected move completes).
(3) Right Shoulder Low = the last dip support before the neckline break. Strong support on any pullback.
(4) Head Low = the absolute floor of the pattern. Any return below the Head = pattern invalidated and new major support becomes active.

---

**2 — Double Bottom**

Geometry: Two consecutive lows at approximately the same price level (within 2–3% of each other), separated by a recovery rally. The rally high between the two lows = the neckline. Confirmed by body close above the neckline.
Signal: Strong bullish reversal. Price tested the same support level twice and both times found aggressive buyers at that exact price. The equal lows = double SSL (strong liquidity pool). On the second test, sellers committed maximum effort and still could not break below — demand is genuine and persistent at the double-bottom price.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Double Bottom LOW (the shared low price) = ultra-strong support. Two tests at the same level = the most significant single support reference the pattern creates.
(2) Neckline = key resistance-turned-support on breakout. The standard retest level.
(3) Measured move target = Neckline + (Neckline − Bottom). Future resistance target.

---

**3 — Triple Bottom**

Geometry: Three tests of the same support level, three equal or near-equal lows, two recovery rallies between them. Third low = final capitulation attempt by sellers. Confirmed by neckline break (the highest of the two rally peaks).
Signal: Even stronger than Double Bottom. Three separate tests of the same price = triple confirmation that demand exhausts all selling there. By the third test, sellers have been thoroughly depleted.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Triple Bottom LOW = the strongest support in the pattern — three-touch confirmation (equivalent to 3-touch EQL in SMC).
(2) Neckline = resistance-turned-support.
(3) Measured move target.
(4) Each individual rally high between the three lows = intermediate resistance levels.

---

**4 — Rounding Bottom (Saucer)**

Geometry: Gradual, curved transition from downtrend to uptrend over an extended period. The price forms a smooth arc (like the inside of a bowl) — no sharp V-reversal, just a slow, methodical rounding. The lip of the saucer (the resistance level at the beginning and end of the arc) = the neckline. Breakout above the lip = confirmation.
Signal: Strong bullish reversal with the highest reliability among reversal patterns because of its duration — the extended basing period represents prolonged institutional accumulation. No sharp moves = no traps, just steady systematic buying over weeks or months.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Saucer lip / neckline = the primary breakout S/R level.
(2) Saucer floor (the lowest point of the arc) = the ultimate support floor.
(3) Measured target = from lip upward by the depth of the arc.

---

**5 — Falling Wedge (Reversal at Bottom)**

Geometry: Two converging downward-sloping trendlines. Both the upper resistance line and the lower support line slope DOWNWARD, but the lower line has a SHALLOWER slope than the upper line — they converge toward a point. Price makes lower highs AND lower lows, but the lows are getting "less low" compared to the highs. The narrowing channel = declining selling momentum.
Signal: Bullish reversal (when at the bottom of a downtrend). Despite making lower prices, sellers are losing power — each push down goes less far. Buyers are progressively absorbing more of each sell-off. The breakout above the upper trendline = sellers finally exhausted.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Upper wedge trendline = the primary resistance line. Breakout above it = confirmation level. After breakout, this line becomes support.
(2) Wedge apex (converging point projection) = a key reference.
(3) Lower wedge line = dynamic support throughout the pattern.
(4) Wedge entry point (widest part at top) = the full pattern height = measured target.

---

**6 — Diamond Bottom**

Geometry: Begins as a Broadening Pattern (expanding price swings) then transitions into a Symmetrical Triangle (converging). The overall shape = a diamond. The left half = expanding swings. The right half = contracting swings. The pattern forms at the bottom of a downtrend.
Signal: Bullish reversal. The broadening phase = seller exhaustion with wild swings. The contracting phase = buyers gradually asserting control. The combination = sellers tried to drive wider and wider but eventually buyers brought them under control.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Upper right diamond trendline breakout = confirmation.
(2) Diamond widest point = the critical reference (highest high of the left half, lowest low of the left half).
(3) Measured target from the breakout point by the height of the diamond.

---

**7 — Broadening Bottom (Inverted Megaphone)**

Geometry: Two DIVERGING trendlines. The lower support line slopes DOWNWARD, the upper resistance line slopes UPWARD. Price swings get WIDER over time (expanding). Forms at the bottom of a trend.
Signal: Bullish reversal. The expanding pattern = increasing volatility as bulls and bears fight more aggressively. The final low (where sellers pushed their furthest) is the reversal point. Pattern confirms on breakout above the upper resistance line.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Upper resistance trendline = primary breakout S/R.
(2) The final (lowest) swing low = the ultimate support floor.
(3) Pattern measured target = upward by height of widest swing.

---

**8 — Cup and Handle**

Geometry: A Rounding Bottom (the "cup") followed by a brief sideways-to-slightly-down consolidation (the "handle"). The handle = a small pullback from the cup's right lip, typically in the upper third of the cup. The handle forms a small downward flag or pennant. Confirmed by breakout above the cup lip (neckline) from the handle consolidation.
Signal: Strong bullish continuation or reversal. The cup = accumulation phase. The handle = a final shakeout of weak longs before the true breakout. Institutions allow price to dip slightly in the handle to trigger stops, absorb them, then launch. The handle formation is the final manipulation before the real move.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Cup lip / neckline = primary breakout S/R. The most important level in the pattern.
(2) Handle low = the pullback support. Strong demand level (institutional absorbed all the handle selling here).
(3) Cup bottom = the deepest accumulation zone = ultra-strong support floor.
(4) Measured move target = neckline + cup depth.

---

**9 — Adam and Eve Double Bottom**

Geometry: A specific Double Bottom variant where the two lows have DIFFERENT shapes. Adam = the first bottom = a sharp V-shaped spike low (one-candle or very short formation). Eve = the second bottom = a wider, more rounded bottom (multiple bars, more gradual).
Signal: The combination of a sharp spike (Adam, potential false breakdown / stop-hunt) + a rounded base (Eve, accumulation) = one of the most reliable double bottom variants. The Adam spike = the stop-hunt that swept SSL. The Eve = patient accumulation. Together they confirm both a liquidity sweep and genuine buying.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Shared low between Adam and Eve = the absolute floor.
(2) Neckline = primary breakout S/R.
(3) Adam spike low = the stop-hunt / sweep level (significant SSL cleared).

---

**10 — Island Reversal Bullish**

Geometry: Price makes a sharp gap DOWN (gap below the previous bar's low), trades sideways or makes a low for one or more bars, then gaps back UP (above the gap-down level). The one or more bars between the two gaps are "island" — completely isolated by gaps on both sides.
Signal: Strong bullish reversal. Two gaps — the gap down = exhaustion of sellers / stop-raid, the gap up = institutional buying so aggressive it gapped price back above the entire selloff zone. The island bars = the absolute low where the reversal originated.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Island LOW (the lowest price of the isolated bars) = ultra-strong support. The most extreme oversold point of the formation.
(2) Gap zones on both sides = FVG-equivalent levels. The gap-up zone becomes support. The gap-down zone becomes resistance.
(3) The entire island range = an exhaustion and reversal zone.

---

**11 — Bump and Run Reversal Bullish (BARR)**

Geometry: Three phases. Lead-in phase: normal upward sloping channel (slope 30–45°). Bump phase: price accelerates sharply (slope > 45°, sometimes > 60°) — a "bump" above the lead-in channel. Run phase: price rolls over and "runs" back down through the lead-in trendline. BULLISH = this at the BOTTOM of a downtrend (Bump downward then Run upward).
Signal: At a bottom, the Bump Down = the final capitulation move — sellers accelerated past the lead-in slope in a panic. After the bump, sellers exhausted, price reverses (the "Run" back upward through the lead-in trendline).
Key S/R levels:
(1) Lead-in channel lower boundary = the key breakout S/R (when price returns above it after the bump = bullish).
(2) Bump extreme low = the ultimate support floor.
(3) Lead-in channel upper boundary = resistance target after breakout.

---

**12 — V-Bottom**

Geometry: A single, sharp, deep decline followed immediately by an equally sharp recovery — forming a V-shape with no base formation. The recovery mirrors the decline in both speed and magnitude.
Signal: Extreme bullish reversal — often driven by a news event, flash crash, or extreme stop-hunt. The symmetry of the V = institutional buy program absorbed the entire selloff immediately. No period of indecision — straight reversal.
Key S/R levels:
(1) V-Bottom LOW = the most significant support level. The precise price where institutional buying overwhelmed all selling in a single session.
(2) The pre-V high (the peak before the V started) = the recovery target and interim resistance.

#### BEARISH REVERSAL (12 patterns)

---

**13 — Head and Shoulders (H&S)**

Geometry: Three-peak formation. Left Shoulder (first high) → Head (highest point — the highest peak of the pattern) → Right Shoulder (third peak, approximately equal to Left Shoulder and LOWER than the Head). Neckline connects the valleys between peaks (the support lows between Left Shoulder/Head and Head/Right Shoulder). Body close BELOW neckline = confirmed.
Signal: Strongest bearish reversal pattern. The Head = the absolute high with maximum buying pressure. The Right Shoulder = a failed attempt to reach the Head — buyers weakened. The neckline break = buyers definitively overcome. Standard institutional distribution completion pattern.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Neckline = primary S/R. Before breakout = support. After breakout = resistance on retest (the most traded level).
(2) Measured move target = Neckline − (Head High − Neckline). Future support at the projected target.
(3) Right Shoulder High = the last rally resistance before the neckline break.
(4) Head High = the absolute ceiling. Any recovery above the Head = pattern invalidated.

---

**14 — Double Top**

Mirror of Double Bottom at a high. Two consecutive highs at approximately the same price, separated by a pullback low (the neckline). Confirmed by body close below the neckline.
Signal: Strong bearish reversal. Two tests of the same resistance = dual BSL confirmation — buyers tried twice and both times sellers absorbed all buying at that exact price.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Double Top HIGH = ultra-strong resistance.
(2) Neckline = support-turned-resistance on breakdown.
(3) Measured target = Neckline − (High − Neckline). Future support level.

---

**15 — Triple Top**

Three tests of the same resistance level. Third high = final attempt by buyers. Three-touch BSL exhaustion.
Key S/R levels: Triple Top HIGH = strongest ceiling (three-touch confirmation). Neckline = support-turned-resistance. Measured target projection.

---

**16 — Rounding Top**

Gradual curved transition from uptrend to downtrend. Smooth arc like the outside of a bowl. The lip = neckline. Extended distribution over time = highest reliability bearish reversal.
Key S/R levels: Rounding Top lip / neckline = primary breakdown S/R. Apex (highest point of arc) = ceiling. Depth target from the lip downward.

---

**17 — Rising Wedge (Reversal at Top)**

Two converging UPWARD-sloping trendlines. Upper resistance slopes up more steeply than lower support. Converging = declining buying momentum. At top of uptrend = reversal signal.
Signal: Bearish reversal. Price making higher highs but the gains are shrinking — buyers losing power progressively. Breakdown below lower wedge line = confirmation.
Key S/R levels: Lower wedge line = primary breakdown S/R. After break, becomes resistance. Wedge entry point = full pattern height = measured target downward.

---

**18 — Diamond Top**

Geometry at a high: Broadening (expanding swings) then contracting into a symmetrical triangle. Diamond shape at the top of an uptrend.
Signal: Bearish reversal. Expanding phase = seller pressure building. Contracting phase = buyers losing control. Breakdown through lower-right diamond line = confirmation.
Key S/R levels: Lower right diamond line = breakdown S/R. Widest point = the maximum extent of the pattern. Measured target downward.

---

**19 — Broadening Top (Megaphone)**

Two diverging trendlines: upper resistance slopes UP, lower support slopes DOWN. Expanding price swings at the top of an uptrend.
Signal: Bearish reversal. Expanding volatility = distribution phase. Sellers and buyers fighting wider and wider, but price is at a high = institutional distribution into retail buying.
Key S/R levels: Lower support trendline = breakdown level. Final swing high = the distribution ceiling.

---

**20 — Inverted Cup and Handle**

Mirror of Cup and Handle at a top. Inverted cup (rounding top) + small upward handle consolidation + breakdown below the cup lip.
Signal: Bearish reversal / distribution completion. The handle = final squeeze of shorts before the real breakdown.
Key S/R levels: Cup lip (inverted) = primary breakdown S/R. Handle HIGH = the last resistance before breakdown. Cup top = the absolute ceiling. Measured target downward.

---

**21 — Eve and Adam Double Top**

Specific Double Top variant. Eve = wider, rounded first top (distribution). Adam = sharp V-shaped second top (exhaustion spike).
Signal: Eve distributes to smart money. Adam = final trap for late longs (BSL sweep). The combination = distribution + stop-raid confirmation.
Key S/R levels: Shared high area = ceiling. Adam spike high = BSL sweep level. Neckline = breakdown S/R.

---

**22 — Island Reversal Bearish**

Price gaps UP, forms island bars at the top, then gaps back DOWN. Island isolated on both sides by gaps.
Signal: Institutional distribution so fast it gapped price back below the entire rally. The island high = the distribution peak.
Key S/R levels: Island HIGH = ultra-strong resistance. Both gap zones = FVG-equivalent levels. Gap-down zone becomes resistance.

---

**23 — Bump and Run Reversal Bearish (BARR)**

At a top. Lead-in channel upward → Bump phase (price accelerates above lead-in slope) → Run phase (price falls back through the lead-in lower trendline).
Signal: The bump = buying acceleration trap. FOMO buyers piled in on the steep slope. After the bump exhausts, the Run = rapid selloff back through the lead-in. All the FOMO buyers from the bump are trapped.
Key S/R levels: Lead-in upper boundary = the key resistance (the "blown" level). Bump extreme high = the absolute top. Lead-in lower boundary = the target S/R.

---

**24 — V-Top**

Sharp rally immediately followed by equally sharp decline. Mirror V at a high. Flash reversal.
Key S/R levels: V-Top HIGH = strongest resistance. Pre-V low = the support target.

### ═══ CATEGORY 2 — Continuation Patterns (20 types) ═══

#### BULLISH CONTINUATION (8 patterns)

---

**25 — Bull Flag**

Geometry: A sharp, near-vertical bullish move (the "flagpole" — impulse). Then a brief, parallel channel of shallow pullback going slightly DOWNWARD or sideways (the "flag" — consolidation). The flag's parallel trendlines slope mildly against the trend. Breakout above the upper flag trendline = continuation confirmed.
Signal: Strong bullish continuation. The flagpole = institutional impulse (large buy). The flag = brief consolidation and shakeout of weak longs (stops below the lower flag line are swept). The breakout = institutional re-entry for the next leg up.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Upper flag trendline = the breakout level. After break → becomes support.
(2) Lower flag trendline = the shakeout support (the level where weak hands were stopped out = strong institutional demand).
(3) Flagpole base = the origin of the impulse = ultra-strong support. The entire flagpole's bottom = where the institutional buy program began.
(4) Measured target = breakout point + flagpole length. The target level enters the pool as future resistance.

---

**26 — Bull Pennant**

Geometry: Sharp bullish flagpole + converging symmetrical triangle consolidation (not parallel like a flag — converging = tightening). The pennant's two trendlines converge to a point.
Signal: Bullish continuation. The convergence = even tighter compression than a flag. Energy builds as the range narrows. The breakout = explosive continuation.
Key S/R levels: Upper pennant line = breakout S/R. Lower pennant line = the support floor of the consolidation. Flagpole base = origin support. Target = breakout + flagpole.

---

**27 — Ascending Triangle**

Geometry: FLAT horizontal upper resistance line (buyers repeatedly hitting the same resistance and holding) + upward-sloping lower support line (buyers making progressively HIGHER lows). The two lines converge at a point.
Signal: Strong bullish continuation (or reversal at bottom). The higher lows = buyers getting more aggressive with each pullback — willing to buy at higher and higher prices. The flat ceiling = sellers defending the same level. Eventually buyers overwhelm → breakout above the flat resistance.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Flat upper resistance line = the primary breakout level. After breakout → this becomes key support.
(2) Upward-sloping lower line = dynamic support throughout the pattern.
(3) Pattern height (widest vertical distance) = measured target above breakout.
(4) Triangle apex = the converging point (deadline for the breakout to occur).

---

**28 — Bull Rectangle**

Geometry: Price consolidates in a horizontal channel with a clear FLAT upper resistance and FLAT lower support. Multiple touches on both sides. The channel moves sideways rather than contracting or expanding.
Signal: Bullish continuation (in uptrend context). The rectangle = a resting zone for institutional accumulation within the trend. Price oscillates between the two levels as institutions fill their orders. The breakout above the upper boundary = accumulation complete, trend resumes.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Upper boundary = the primary breakout S/R. After break → support.
(2) Lower boundary = the strong support floor of the consolidation.
(3) Rectangle midpoint = equilibrium within the pattern.
(4) Measured target = breakout + rectangle height.

---

**29 — Bull Channel**

Geometry: Two parallel upward-sloping trendlines containing price. Upper line = resistance. Lower line = support. Price oscillates between them in an orderly uptrend.
Signal: Bullish continuation. The channel defines the trend's boundaries. Each touch of the lower channel line = institutional re-entry point. Each touch of the upper line = short-term resistance.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Lower channel trendline = dynamic support. Each touch of this line = a buy S/R level.
(2) Upper channel trendline = dynamic resistance / continuation target.
(3) Channel midline (equidistant between upper and lower) = the equilibrium — price frequently reacts here.
(4) Channel breakout levels — if price breaks above the upper channel = acceleration signal.

---

**30 — Measured Move Up**

Geometry: Two equal (or approximately equal) bullish legs separated by a consolidation/correction phase. Leg 1 (impulse) → Correction → Leg 2 (same length as Leg 1). The "measured move" principle = Leg 2 will travel the same distance as Leg 1.
Signal: Bullish continuation with a defined target. The two-leg structure represents: (1) initial institutional impulse, (2) distribution/consolidation as some institutions take profit, (3) second institutional impulse from the same institutional program completing its target.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Correction low = the consolidation support (the pullback support between the two legs = strong demand zone).
(2) Leg 1 origin = the base from which the entire measured move is calculated.
(3) Projected Leg 2 target = Correction Low + Leg 1 Length = the most significant level — where the second impulse is expected to exhaust. This target = future resistance.

---

**31 — Falling Wedge (Continuation in Uptrend)**

Same geometry as Pattern 5 (Falling Wedge), but occurring MID-TREND during a bullish trend — a corrective falling wedge within an uptrend.
Signal: Bullish continuation (not reversal). The wedge = a pullback structure where sellers are losing power within the correction. The breakout above the upper wedge line = trend resumes.
Key S/R levels: Same as Pattern 5. Upper wedge line = breakout S/R. Lower wedge line = dynamic support. Wedge base = origin support.

---

**32 — Three-Push Bull**

Geometry: Three successive bullish pushes upward, each reaching a higher high, with each push having progressively LESS MOMENTUM (each push is slightly smaller than the previous, each pullback goes slightly deeper). The third push = the weakest.
Signal: Bullish continuation pattern BUT with exhaustion warning. The three pushes confirm the trend is continuing. However the momentum divergence (each push weaker) = the trend may be losing steam after the third push. Often the third push's high = the key inflection point for either a strong continuation or a reversal.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Third push HIGH = the most significant level — the exhaustion point. Either continues strongly from here or reverses.
(2) First push origin = the base of the three-push structure.
(3) Each pullback low between the three pushes = a sub-level support series.

#### NEUTRAL CONTINUATION (4 patterns)

---

**41 — Symmetrical Triangle**

Geometry: Upper DOWNWARD-sloping resistance line + lower UPWARD-sloping support line. Converging toward a point. Price makes lower highs AND higher lows simultaneously — classic compression. The breakout direction determines the pattern's bias.
Signal: Neutral — continuation in the direction of the breakout. Most commonly breaks in the direction of the prevailing trend (continuation), but can reverse. The compression = building tension.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Upper trendline = primary resistance. Breakout above = bullish trigger (becomes support).
(2) Lower trendline = primary support. Breakdown below = bearish trigger (becomes resistance).
(3) Triangle apex = the convergence point. Price must break before reaching the apex (patterns lose reliability past 75% of apex distance).
(4) Pattern height at the widest point = the measured target distance from the breakout.

---

**42 — Expanding Triangle (Broadening Formation)**

Two DIVERGING trendlines. Upper line slopes UP, lower line slopes DOWN. Price swings get wider over time. The opposite of a Symmetrical Triangle.
Signal: Neutral continuation with increasing volatility. The expanding swings = indecision growing. Price cannot find direction. Typically resolves in the direction of the prior trend.
Key S/R levels: Upper trendline = resistance swing reference. Lower trendline = support swing reference. The widest extent levels = the outer boundaries of the volatility zone.

---

**43 — Rectangle (Neutral)**

Horizontal consolidation with flat top and flat bottom. No slope on either side. Pure balance between supply and demand.
Signal: Neutral — continuation in breakout direction. The rectangle = a pause where neither side has an edge. Traders use the upper boundary as short and lower as long until the direction becomes clear.
Key S/R levels: Upper boundary = resistance (short-term). Lower boundary = support (short-term). Rectangle midpoint = the equilibrium. The boundaries become major S/R after the breakout resolves direction.

---

**44 — Coil / Range Compression**

Geometry: Progressively tighter price action — each candle's range narrows. Multiple consecutive inside bars. The "coil" = energy compressing before a spring.
Signal: Neutral — high-velocity breakout in breakout direction. The tighter the compression, the more explosive the eventual move.
Key S/R levels: The HIGH of the entire coil formation = key resistance (breakout triggers above this). The LOW = key support (breakdown triggers below). Coil midpoint = the equilibrium center. The first strong candle after the coil defines the direction and becomes the reference for the subsequent move.

### ═══ CATEGORY 3 — Structural Price Patterns (4 types) ═══

---

**45 — Wolfe Wave Bullish**

Geometry: 5-wave structure. Wave 1-2 = first swing. Wave 2-3 = second swing (higher than 1). Wave 3-4 = third swing. Wave 4-5 = the final decline that typically overshoots (goes BELOW the 1-3 trendline). The "sweet zone" for buying = when price touches or exceeds the 1-3 trendline at point 5. Target = the 1-4 trendline projected forward (the "EPA" line — Estimated Price at Arrival).
Signal: Strong bullish reversal at Point 5. The overshoot of the 1-3 trendline = the stop-hunt / false breakdown. Price then drives toward the EPA target.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Point 5 LOW = the entry/reversal level. Strong support (the overshoot low).
(2) Point 1-3 trendline = the breakdown zone and then the pivot.
(3) EPA trendline projection (1-4 line extended) = the target S/R level. This is where the wave completes.

---

**46 — Wolfe Wave Bearish**

Mirror. 5-wave structure with an overshoot ABOVE the 1-3 trendline at Point 5 = the BSL sweep. Target = 1-4 line projected downward.
Key S/R levels: Point 5 HIGH = the reversal/distribution level. 1-3 line = the breakout overshoot zone. EPA projection = downside target S/R.

---

**47 — ABCD Pattern Bullish**

Geometry: AB leg (bearish impulse down) → BC leg (retracement up, 61.8–78.6% of AB) → CD leg (bearish impulse, equal length or 127.2–161.8% of BC) that completes at the "D" point. The D point = the PRZ (Potential Reversal Zone).
Signal: Bullish reversal at D. The CD leg is a second bearish impulse that mirrors AB — but at D, the pattern is complete and price reverses up. The Fibonacci ratios (BC = 61.8–78.6% of AB, CD = 127.2–161.8% of BC) define the precise reversal zone.
Key S/R levels:
(1) D point = the primary reversal S/R level — where the pattern completes and buyers are expected to overwhelm sellers.
(2) B point = intermediate resistance on the recovery.
(3) The Fibonacci confluence within the PRZ zone (where multiple ratios converge) = the most precise sub-level within D.

---

**48 — ABCD Pattern Bearish**

Mirror. AB bullish impulse → BC retracement down → CD bullish impulse completing at D = bearish PRZ.
Key S/R levels: D point = distribution/reversal level. B point = intermediate support. PRZ = precise resistance zone.

### ═══ EXTENSION 3: State Tracking (Forming / Confirmed / Retesting / Target Active / Invalidated ×5) ═══

**What it is (Count-Contributing):** Five-phase lifecycle unique to chart patterns — more complex than candle patterns because chart patterns have a neckline, a retest, AND a target.

**Forming state (weight: 0.60×):**
The pattern is still developing. All structural components (shoulders, triangle lines, flag body) may not be complete. The neckline is identified but not yet broken. S/R levels from the forming pattern (the component highs, lows, neckline) are plotted but flagged with lower weight — the pattern hasn't proven itself yet. Trading the forming state = anticipating the breakout, not confirming it.

**Confirmed state (weight: 1.0×):**
Price body-closed beyond the primary breakout boundary. For H&S: body close below neckline. For Bull Flag: body close above upper flag trendline. For Ascending Triangle: body close above flat resistance.
- The neckline/breakout level flips function (was resistance → now support, or was support → now resistance)
- The measured move target is now added to the S/R pool as a live target level
- All component sub-levels (shoulder highs, individual triangle touches) are fully active

**Retesting state (weight: 1.15×, elevated):**
After the confirmed breakout, price PULLS BACK to retest the neckline/breakout level. This is the highest-conviction state for the pattern's primary S/R level — the retest is when the flip of function is PROVEN in real time. A neckline that holds on retest = definitively confirmed as the new support/resistance.
- The retest of the neckline is a distinct traded level that generates the second-most-significant S/R event of the pattern lifecycle
- Weight elevated because a successful retest is the most institutionally significant moment
- Failed retest (price closes through the neckline during the retest) → Invalidated

**Target Active state (weight: 1.0× for primary, 0.90× for neckline):**
The neckline held the retest. Price is now progressing toward the measured move target. The target level becomes the DOMINANT active S/R level from the pattern (resistance for bearish patterns, support for bullish patterns awaiting the target).
- The original neckline S/R level remains active but at reduced weight (0.90×) — it has served its flip-purpose
- The target level is now at full weight and drawing price toward it

**Invalidated state (weight: 0.0 for all levels; failure levels created):**
Pattern failed — price closed back through the neckline in the wrong direction after confirmation. All pattern levels removed from the main pool. The failure creates a new S/R level (Extension 9).

### ═══ EXTENSION 6: Volume Profile on Breakout (Attribute) ═══

### ═══ EXTENSION 7: Internal Structure Sub-Levels (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** Every chart pattern contains internal price levels that become independent S/R references beyond just the neckline and target. These sub-levels are added to the pool individually.

**By pattern type:**

**Head and Shoulders:**
- Left Shoulder High = resistance reference (interim high on the way up)
- Right Shoulder High = the last lower-high = resistance
- Head High = the absolute ceiling

**Ascending Triangle:**
- Each touch point on the flat upper resistance line = individual BSL reference

- Triangle midline (equidistant between upper and lower at any given point) = equilibrium
= Variable count based on touch count

**Double Bottom:**
- Each bottom low (individually) = support sub-level
- The swing high between the two bottoms (the neckline point) = resistance sub-level
= 3 sub-levels

**Bull Flag:**
- Upper flag trendline lower contact points = resistance sub-references within the flag
- Lower flag trendline upper contact points = support sub-references
- Flag midline = equilibrium within the consolidation
= Variable based on flag length

### ═══ EXTENSION 9: Pattern Failure Level (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** A confirmed chart pattern that subsequently FAILS (price closes back through the neckline after breakout) creates one of the highest-probability S/R levels in technical analysis — the "failed pattern trap level."

**Mechanism:**
- D1 H&S breaks below neckline (Confirmed). Traders go short. Stops placed above the right shoulder.
- Price subsequently closes BACK ABOVE the neckline (Invalidated).
- All the short traders who entered on the H&S breakout are now trapped — their stops above the right shoulder become BUY orders.
- Additionally, the failed pattern signals to institutions that the supposed distribution is exhausted — bullish.
- Result: The RIGHT SHOULDER HIGH (where stops of trapped shorts cluster) becomes a BSL target — and after being swept, the area becomes strong support.

**Failure level creation rules:**
When `pattern.state == INVALIDATED`:
```
If(pattern.type == BEARISH_REVERSAL) {
   failure_level.price = pattern.right_shoulder_high OR pattern.pattern_high;
   failure_level.direction = BULLISH;
   failure_level.weight = pattern.confirmed_weight × 0.80;
   failure_level.type = FAILURE_TRAP_BULL;
}
If(pattern.type == BULLISH_REVERSAL) {
   failure_level.price = pattern.right_shoulder_low OR pattern.pattern_low;
   failure_level.direction = BEARISH;
   failure_level.weight = pattern.confirmed_weight × 0.80;
   failure_level.type = FAILURE_TRAP_BEAR;
}
```

**The "head fake" trade:** Pattern confirms → fails → failure level becomes the new S/R → price drives to failure level. This is a known institutional pattern: let retail trade the setup, stop them out, then drive to the failure level where additional stops have clustered. Tracking failure levels systematically captures these institutional "trap runs."

### ═══ EXTENSION 11: Neckline Retest as Independent S/R Event (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** The neckline retest after a confirmed breakout is the single most reliable S/R event in chart pattern analysis. It deserves its own tracking layer separate from the standard state machine.

**Why the retest is exceptional:**
When a confirmed breakout pulls back to retest the neckline:
- All traders who traded the breakout are watching this level
- Institutions that distributed/accumulated know where the flip-level is
- The retest is the REAL TEST of whether the pattern's breakout was genuine
- A neckline that holds on retest (price rejects from the old resistance now acting as support) = the highest conviction S/R proof in pattern analysis

**Retest sub-states:**
```
RETESTING_APPROACH   → price is within 10 pips of neckline from the correct side
RETESTING_TOUCH      → price wicked into neckline but body hasn't closed through
RETESTING_HOLD       → body closed AWAY from neckline (held → transition to Target Active)
RETESTING_FAIL       → body closed THROUGH neckline (→ Invalidated + Failure Level created)
```

**Enhanced weight during retest:**
While in RETESTING state:
- Neckline level weight = 1.15× (elevated — this is the "moment of truth" for the level)
- If `RETESTING_HOLD` confirmed: neckline weight permanently elevated to 1.10× for remainder of pattern lifetime (proven flip point)
- `RETESTING_FAIL` → failure level created at weight = confirmed_weight × 0.80

**Visual distinction:** Neckline in RETESTING state = displayed differently (e.g., thicker line, different color) to alert trader that the critical retest is occurring NOW.

### ═══ EXTENSION 12: Pattern-Within-Pattern (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** When a SMALLER chart pattern forms at or near a key level of a LARGER chart pattern, the confluence of two independent pattern formations at the same price = exceptional S/R significance.

**Common fractal formations:**

**Flag within H&S:**

- Weight: H&S neckline weight + pattern-within-pattern bonus +0.5

**Triangle at a neckline:**

- The triangle at the neckline = maximum compression + neckline = the most significant decision point.
- Weight: both patterns' neckline weights combined → maximum confluence

- Pattern-within-pattern bonus: +0.4

**Cup within Cup:**
