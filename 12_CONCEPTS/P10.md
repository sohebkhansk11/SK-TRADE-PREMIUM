### ═══ CATEGORY 1 — Single Candle Patterns (13 core) ═══

---

**1 — Standard Doji**

Geometry: Open ≈ Close — both values within 5–10% of the total candle range. Upper and lower wicks both present and of approximately equal length. The body is a thin horizontal line near the middle of the range.
Signal: Maximum indecision — buyers and sellers fought with exactly equal force and neither won. Context determines direction: at resistance = bearish lean, at support = bullish lean. The Doji alone is not a signal — it is a pause that requires the next candle to resolve direction.
Key S/R levels created: Doji HIGH (resistance reference) and Doji LOW (support reference). Both levels enter the pool. The full range of the Doji = the indecision/decision zone.

---

**2 — Gravestone Doji**

Geometry: Open = Close = Low (or within a few ticks). Very long upper wick — price rallied significantly during the session but closed exactly where it opened (at the low). No meaningful lower wick.
Signal: Bearish reversal candle. The narrative: buyers drove price all the way up (the rally is the upper wick), but sellers overwhelmed them completely by the close, returning price to the open. Every buyer during the session is now at breakeven or worse. Best at resistance zones.
Key S/R level: Gravestone HIGH = the strongest resistance reference — that is the exact price level where sellers absorbed all buying pressure and reversed the session. The wick extreme = where demand was completely consumed.

---

**3 — Dragonfly Doji**

Geometry: Open = Close = High (or within a few ticks). Very long lower wick — price sold off significantly during the session but recovered completely to close at the high. No meaningful upper wick.
Signal: Bullish reversal candle. Sellers drove price down aggressively (the lower wick), but buyers overwhelmed all of that selling and closed at the top. Every seller during the session is at breakeven or worse. Best at support zones.
Key S/R level: Dragonfly LOW = the strongest support reference. That is where buyers entered en masse and overwhelmed all selling. The wick low = exact demand activation point.

---

**4 — Long-Legged Doji**

Geometry: Open ≈ Close near the middle of the range. Both upper AND lower wicks are very long (typically each > 40% of total range). Price moved dramatically in both directions during the session and ended near where it started.
Signal: Extreme indecision with HIGH VOLATILITY. Both buyers and sellers fought aggressively and violently — neither side won, but both sides tried hard. This typically precedes a significant directional move — the market is building energy at a decision point.
Key S/R levels: The HIGH (where sellers stopped the rally) and the LOW (where buyers stopped the decline) both enter the pool. The full range = a defined "battle zone." Whichever boundary price closes through on the next candle = the directional break.

---

**5 — Hammer**

Geometry: Small body in the UPPER quarter of the candle range. Long lower wick — minimum 2× the body length, preferably 3×+. Minimal or no upper wick. Forms at the bottom of a downtrend or at a support level.
Signal: Strong bullish reversal. The narrative: sellers drove price far down (long wick), but buyers overwhelmed them so forcefully they closed the candle near the top. The lower wick length literally represents the RANGE OVER WHICH BUYERS DOMINATED SELLERS. A perfect Hammer (no upper wick, body at the very top, wick 3×+ body) = maximum institutional buying at that price range.
Key S/R level — TWO levels:
(1) Hammer LOW = the primary support level. This is where buyers entered most aggressively — the absolute floor of buying demand.
(2) Hammer HIGH = the confirmation trigger. Once next candle body-closes above this level, the reversal is confirmed. After confirmation, this HIGH = support on any retracement.

---

**6 — Inverted Hammer**

Geometry: Small body in the LOWER quarter of the candle. Long UPPER wick (2×+ body). Minimal or no lower wick. Forms at the bottom of a downtrend (same position as Hammer but upside down).
Signal: Bullish reversal — but REQUIRES confirmation from the next candle. The upper wick shows buyers made an attempt to push price higher. They didn't maintain it (body closed near the low), but the attempt itself signals buying interest is emerging. Without a confirming bullish candle closing above the Inverted Hammer's high → the signal is not activated.
Key S/R level: Inverted Hammer HIGH = the key level. If the next candle closes above it (confirmation), the HIGH becomes the primary breakout support reference. Before confirmation → it's a potential resistance level to watch.

---

**7 — Shooting Star**

Geometry: Small body in the LOWER quarter. Long upper wick (2×+ body). Minimal lower wick. Forms at the TOP of an uptrend or at a resistance level. (Looks identical to Inverted Hammer, but position and context are bearish.)
Signal: Strong bearish reversal. Buyers drove price significantly higher during the session (the long upper wick), but sellers completely overwhelmed them and closed the candle near the bottom. The upper wick = the range where buyers were active but sellers fully absorbed all that buying. Every buyer during the session is trapped.
Key S/R level: Shooting Star HIGH = strong resistance. The wick extreme is where sellers overpowered all buying — institutional supply level. Confirmed by the following bearish candle.

---

**8 — Hanging Man**

Geometry: Small body in the UPPER quarter. Long lower wick (2×+ body). Minimal upper wick. Forms at the TOP of an uptrend. (Looks identical to Hammer, but context is bearish.)
Signal: Bearish reversal warning — requires confirmation. The long lower wick at a HIGH shows selling pressure appeared during the session. Bulls recovered by the close (body near the top), but the selling pressure existed and could re-emerge. Context: price already at a high = the selling pressure that appeared mid-session is a warning.
Key S/R level: Hanging Man BODY LOW = the level where selling emerged. Also: Hanging Man LOW = the wick extreme where selling pressure was most intense. If next candle body-closes below the Hanging Man LOW → bearish confirmation.

---

**9 — Bullish Marubozu**

Geometry: OPEN = LOW (or within 1–2 ticks). CLOSE = HIGH (or within 1–2 ticks). Entire candle is a full body — no wicks, or negligible wicks. Every tick from open to close was bullish — buyers were in complete control the entire session.
Signal: Extreme bullish momentum. Not a reversal pattern — it is a continuation/momentum pattern. A full Marubozu means not a single moment of the session saw sellers dominate. Used to confirm trend direction, break-out validation, or institutional one-way delivery.
Key S/R levels — STRONG:
(1) Marubozu OPEN (= Low) = strong support. This is where the institutional buying began — from the very first tick. The open of a Bullish Marubozu = level where institutions entered the session with aggressive buy orders.
(2) Marubozu CLOSE (= High) = breakout level / secondary support on retracement.
(3) Entire Marubozu BODY = institutional demand zone (any part of the body = support on retracement).

---

**10 — Bearish Marubozu**

Mirror of #9. OPEN = HIGH, CLOSE = LOW. Full bearish body.
Signal: Extreme bearish momentum — sellers in complete control every tick.
Key S/R: Marubozu OPEN (= High) = strong resistance (where institutional selling began). Entire body = supply zone.

---

**11 — Spinning Top**

Geometry: Small body (< 25% of total range) located approximately in the MIDDLE of the candle. Upper and lower wicks both present and both meaningful. Total range is wider than the body.
Signal: Indecision — both buyers and sellers were active (hence the wicks) but neither sustained control (hence the small body). Less decisive than a Doji (which has virtually no body). The body color (green/red) gives a very slight edge but not enough to trade on alone.
Key S/R level: Spinning Top HIGH and LOW define a short-term balance zone. The range of the candle = a micro-indecision zone. When price revisits this range, it often spends time inside it before choosing direction.

---

**12 — Bullish Belt Hold**

Geometry: Opens at or near the ABSOLUTE LOW of the session (like the beginning of a Marubozu). Strong bullish body from that open. Has an upper wick but minimal lower wick (< 5% of total range). The defining characteristic: the open IS the low.
Signal: Bullish. From the very first tick of the session, buyers took complete control — not a single moment of downside from the opening price. Different from Marubozu in that the close doesn't have to be the high (can have upper wick), but the open being the low = total initial buyer control.
Key S/R level: Belt Hold OPEN (= Low) = strong support. The level where buyers entered with 100% initial dominance. Any revisit to this level = institutional re-entry area.

---

**13 — Bearish Belt Hold**

Mirror. Opens at or near the absolute HIGH of the session. Strong bearish body from the open. Minimal upper wick.
Key S/R level: Belt Hold OPEN (= High) = strong resistance.

### ═══ CATEGORY 2 — Two-Candle Patterns (8 core) ═══

---

**14 — Bullish Engulfing**

Geometry: Candle 1 = bearish (any size). Candle 2 = bullish candle whose BODY completely engulfs Candle 1's body — Candle 2 opens below Candle 1's close and closes above Candle 1's open. Bodies (not wicks) define the engulf. The larger the ratio of Candle 2 body to Candle 1 body, the stronger.
Signal: Strong bullish reversal. Two-session narrative: (1) sellers in control, (2) buyers overwhelm the ENTIRE previous session's bearish body and then some. This is the two-candle equivalent of a BOS — Candle 2's buyers consumed all of Candle 1's sellers and exceeded their level. Institutional buying absorbed all the previous selling.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Candle 2 LOW = primary support. This is where the buyers opened their position — the floor of the entire bullish response.
(2) Candle 1 LOW = secondary support (the range the sellers established before being overwhelmed).
(3) The engulfing zone (Candle 1's open to Candle 2's low) = the established demand zone going forward.

---

**15 — Bearish Engulfing**

Mirror of #14. Candle 2 bearish body engulfs Candle 1 bullish body.
Key S/R levels: Candle 2 HIGH = primary resistance. Candle 1 HIGH = secondary resistance.

---

**16 — Bullish Harami**

Geometry: Candle 1 = large bearish candle. Candle 2 = small body candle (bullish or neutral) whose ENTIRE BODY fits INSIDE Candle 1's body. "Harami" means pregnant — Candle 2 is "inside" Candle 1.
Signal: Moderate bullish reversal — less strong than Engulfing. The small candle inside the large = sellers lost momentum. The trend is pausing. Best used at support zones or after sustained downtrends. Requires additional context (confluence with S/R level) for full conviction.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Candle 1 LOW = the support zone where the large bearish move ended.
(2) Candle 2 HIGH = the trigger — if the following candle closes above this, bullish confirmation.

---

**17 — Bearish Harami**

Large bullish + small bearish body inside the bullish body.
Key S/R: Candle 1 HIGH = resistance zone. Candle 2 LOW = the bearish trigger.

---

**18 — Tweezer Bottom**

Geometry: Two or more consecutive candles with EQUAL or near-equal LOWS (within 2–3 ticks). Candle 1 typically bearish, Candle 2 bullish (or vice versa — the combination matters less than the equal lows). The two candles both probed the same price low and both were rejected from it.
Signal: Strong bullish reversal. Two separate sessions tested the same price level and both found buying demand at that exact price. The equal lows = a double confirmation of support. Sellers could not push below that level on two attempts — demand is genuinely resting there.
Key S/R level: The SHARED LOW = ultra-strong support. Two sessions touched this price and reversed — this is the twin SSL level. Equivalent to an EQL in SMC context. The confidence in this level is doubled by the twin-test.

---

**19 — Tweezer Top**

Two or more candles with equal or near-equal HIGHS.
Key S/R level: Shared HIGH = ultra-strong resistance. Two sessions hit this price and reversed = twin BSL level.

---

**20 — Piercing Line**

Geometry: Candle 1 = strong bearish candle. Candle 2 = bullish candle that OPENS BELOW Candle 1's close (below the low — gap or aggressive open) and CLOSES ABOVE THE MIDPOINT of Candle 1's body. Critical requirement: close > 50% of the way up into Candle 1's body.
Signal: Bullish reversal. The second candle "pierces" more than halfway into the previous bearish body — buyers reclaimed more than half of the bears' ground in one session. The 50%+ reclaim = buyers demonstrating sustained force (not just a brief bounce).
Key S/R levels:
(1) Candle 2 CLOSE = the key level. This is where buyers proved their strength (closed above the midpoint). This close level = support on retracement.
(2) Candle 1 LOW = the base support floor.

---

**21 — Dark Cloud Cover**

Mirror of Piercing. Candle 2 opens above Candle 1's high and closes below the midpoint of Candle 1's bullish body.
Key S/R: Candle 2 CLOSE (below 50% of bullish body) = resistance going forward. Candle 1 HIGH = the ceiling.

### ═══ CATEGORY 3 — Three-Candle Patterns (5 core) ═══

---

**22 — Morning Star**

Geometry: Candle 1 = large bearish candle (strong downward momentum). Candle 2 = small body candle ("star") — ideally with a gap down from Candle 1's close; small body, can be bullish or bearish; the key is it's small and represents indecision. Candle 3 = large bullish candle that closes ABOVE THE MIDPOINT of Candle 1's body (minimum) — ideally above Candle 1's open.
Signal: Strong bullish reversal. Three-act story: (1) Bears dominate fully, (2) Indecision — bears lose control, market pauses, (3) Bulls take over decisively and reclaim a major portion of the previous decline. This is the three-candle equivalent of a CHoCH — selling exhausted, buyers confirmed.
Key S/R levels:
(1) Candle 2 LOW (star low) = the ultimate reversal pivot. The precise price where the bearish trend halted and buyers emerged.
(2) Candle 1 LOW = the base floor of the pattern.
(3) Candle 3 CLOSE = confirmation level (> midpoint of Candle 1 = confirmed signal). This close level = support on retracement.

---

**23 — Evening Star**

Mirror of Morning Star at the top of an uptrend.
Three-act: (1) Bulls dominate, (2) Indecision star, (3) Bears reclaim > 50% of Bull body.
Key S/R: Candle 2 HIGH = reversal pivot. Candle 3 CLOSE = resistance going forward.

---

**24 — Three White Soldiers**

Geometry: Three consecutive LARGE bullish candles. Each opens within the previous candle's body (not gapping above — orderly progression). Each closes near its own HIGH (minimal upper wicks — sustained buying throughout each session). Each closes HIGHER than the previous. All three bodies are substantial in size.
Signal: Very strong bullish trend. Three sessions of sustained institutional buying with no hesitation, no pullback. This is a buying PROGRAM — not a spike. The methodical, orderly nature (opening within previous body = no gaps) suggests disciplined accumulation.
Key S/R level: First Soldier OPEN = where the institutional buying program began = strong support. This level is the "launch point" of the three-candle advance. Any retracement back to this level = the institutional entry zone.

---

**25 — Three Black Crows**

Mirror of Three White Soldiers. Three consecutive large bearish candles, each opening within the previous body, each closing near its low, progressively lower.
Signal: Very strong bearish selling program.
Key S/R level: First Crow OPEN = where the institutional selling program launched = strong resistance.

---

**26 — Three Inside Up**

Geometry: Candle 1 = large bearish candle. Candle 2 = small bullish candle inside Candle 1's body (Bullish Harami). Candle 3 = bullish candle that closes ABOVE CANDLE 1'S OPEN (completely negates the first bearish candle).
Signal: Bullish reversal with triple confirmation. (1) Bears showed force, (2) Harami pause, (3) Bulls conclusively prove buyers overwhelmed Day 1's bears by closing above the first candle's open. More reliable than Harami alone because the third candle gives definitive evidence. The third candle's close = breakout confirmation.
Key S/R level: Candle 3 CLOSE = strong support (the level where the three-step confirmation was completed). Candle 1 LOW = the base floor. The three-candle formation LOW = the entire support zone.

**State definitions for candle patterns:**

| State | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **Formed** | Pattern just closed on the last bar. Not yet confirmed by the subsequent candle. |
| **Confirmed** | The candle immediately following the pattern closed in the direction the pattern predicts (e.g., Hammer: next candle closes above Hammer high). The reference levels are now active S/R. |
| **Tested** | Price returned to the pattern's key level after confirmation and respected it — the S/R held. |
| **Invalidated** | Price body-closed THROUGH the pattern's invalidation level (for Hammer: body close below Hammer low; for Shooting Star: body close above Shooting Star high). Level removed from pool. |

### ═══ EXTENSION 1: Full Pattern Expansion (26 → 52 types) ═══

**What it is (Count-Contributing):** The 26 core patterns are the foundation level — what any standard technical analysis course covers. The AI expansion doubles the library to 52 by adding: advanced three-candle variants (Morning Doji Star, Abandoned Baby), kicker patterns, three-line strikes, three methods continuations, separating and counterattack lines, tower and ladder formations, and neck-line patterns. Each of the 26 additional patterns has distinct geometric criteria, a different underlying institutional narrative, and creates different key S/R levels than any existing pattern.

**Why each category of added patterns matters:**
- Kicker patterns (31/32): represent the single most decisive institutional regime change — identified by open-to-open gap (not close-to-open) — the rare occasions where institutions completely change stance overnight. Their S/R levels carry the same weight as a major Wyckoff Spring.
- Three Line Strike (38/39): specifically signals a manipulation reversal — three candles of misdirection followed by one true directional candle. Their lows/highs are the "manipulation base" S/R levels.
- Three Methods (40/41): continuation S/R specifically — the consolidation zone within the pattern = institutional reload area. S/R from continuation patterns is different from reversal pattern S/R.
- Neck patterns (51/52): create "cap levels" — S/R types that are purely about WHERE buyers ran out of steam, rather than where sellers entered. Different institutional logic.

### ═══ EXTENSION 2: Strength Dimension (Strong / Normal / Weak ×3) ═══

**What it is (Count-Contributing):** A Hammer with a body that is 5% of the total range and a wick 20× the body = a completely different institutional statement than a Hammer with a body that is 35% of the range and a wick that barely meets the 2× rule. Same pattern name, vastly different institutional force. Strength grades this.

**Strength criteria by pattern category:**

**Single candle patterns (body-to-range ratio):**

| Grade | Criterion |
|-------|-----------|
| Strong | Candle total range > 1.5 × ATR(14). AND wick-to-body ratio meets premium threshold (e.g., Hammer: lower wick > 3× body). |
| Normal | Range 0.75–1.5 × ATR. Standard wick/body ratios. |
| Weak | Range < 0.75 × ATR. Minimal wick proportions (barely meets pattern criteria). |

**Two/Three-candle patterns (Engulfing size, Star body size):**

| Grade | Criterion |
|-------|-----------|
| Strong | Engulfing candle > 1.5× ATR. Star body < 15% of Candle 1 body (maximum indecision). Three White Soldiers each > 1.0× ATR. |
| Normal | Standard sizes — meets pattern rules comfortably. |
| Weak | Engulfing barely larger than engulfed candle. Star body > 30% of Candle 1. Soldiers with large wicks. |

**Weight multiplier:**
- Strong: × 1.20 on base pattern weight
- Normal: × 1.00
- Weak: × 0.80 (still counted and displayed — weak patterns at strong S/R levels still have confluence value)

**Special strength override:** Any Abandoned Baby (29/30) or Kicker (31/32) pattern is automatically graded STRONG regardless of candle size — their defining characteristic (gap isolation or open-to-open gap) supersedes the ATR criterion.

### ═══ EXTENSION 3: State Tracking (Formed / Confirmed / Tested / Invalidated ×4) ═══

**What it is (Count-Contributing):** Each candle pattern progresses through four distinct phases that determine whether its key S/R levels are active, reliable, or dead.

**FORMED state:**
- Pattern just appeared on the most recently closed candle or candle set
- The SIGNAL is present but NOT yet validated by the next candle
- Weight: 0.75× of confirmed weight (signal present but unconfirmed = lower conviction)
- Display: shown as a "pending" signal — different visual style
- Key levels are plotted but flagged as unconfirmed
- Patterns that require confirmation (Inverted Hammer, Hanging Man, single Doji) stay at FORMED until the confirmation candle closes

**CONFIRMED state:**
- The candle immediately following the pattern closed in the predicted direction
- For Hammer: next candle body-closed above the Hammer high = bullish confirmation
- For Shooting Star: next candle body-closed below the Shooting Star low = bearish confirmation
- For patterns that are self-confirming (Three White Soldiers, Kicker, Abandoned Baby): transition directly from Formed → Confirmed
- Weight: full 1.0× — key levels are fully active S/R in the pool
- Key levels from the pattern (HIGH, LOW, body high, body low) all enter the S/R pool as active levels

**TESTED state:**
- Price returned to the pattern's primary key level after confirmation and HELD (did not body-close through the invalidation level)
- The test confirmed the S/R is genuine — the pattern's prediction proved accurate
- Weight: 1.1× (slight boost — tested and held = proven demand/supply at that level)
- The pattern has "earned" its S/R status through a successful retest

**INVALIDATED state:**
- Price body-closed THROUGH the pattern's invalidation boundary:
  - Bullish patterns: body close below the pattern's LOW
  - Bearish patterns: body close above the pattern's HIGH
- Weight: 0.0 — level removed from pool
- Exception: when a bullish pattern is invalidated (price body-closes below its low), the low level itself transitions to RESISTANCE — an "inverted pattern level" (linked to Extension 9 — Pattern Failure Level)

### ═══ EXTENSION 7: Pattern Proportionality Scoring (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** Beyond just Strong/Normal/Weak grade, each pattern has IDEAL PROPORTIONS that maximize its statistical reliability. Proportionality scoring measures how close a pattern is to its ideal geometry and adjusts weight accordingly.

**By pattern type:**

```

> 4.0: Ideal Proportion score = 1.0 (maximum)

2.0–3.0: Score = 0.70 (minimum acceptable)
< 2.0: Not a valid pattern (fails detection)

Upper wick (for Hammer):
= 0 (no upper wick): +0.10 bonus
> 0 but < 10% of body: acceptable
> 30% of body: −0.10 penalty
```

**Engulfing:**
```
Engulfing ratio = Candle 2 body / Candle 1 body:
> 3.0: Full engulf with room = Score 1.0

1.5–2.0: Normal = 0.70
1.0–1.5: Barely engulfing = 0.55
```

```
Star body as % of Candle 1 body:

10–25%: Good = 0.85

> 40%: Weak star = 0.55

Candle 3 close penetration into Candle 1:
> 75%: Strong reversal confirmation = +0.15 bonus
50–75%: Normal
```

**Why this matters:** A D1 Hammer and H4 Morning Star forming at the same price = two completely independent multi-candle institutional sequences agreeing on the same support level. The probability that this level is genuine support is far higher than either pattern alone.

### ═══ EXTENSION 9: Pattern Failure Level (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** When a confirmed candle pattern is subsequently INVALIDATED — price body-closed through the pattern's key level after the signal was confirmed — the failure itself creates a new S/R level in the OPPOSITE direction. This is the candle pattern equivalent of an iFVG or Breaker Block.

**Mechanism:**

**Scenario:** D1 Bullish Engulfing at 1.0850 (Confirmed state). Expected: price should hold above Candle 2 low (1.0820). Instead, price body-closes below 1.0820 (Invalidated). What this means:
- ALL the buyers who entered based on the bullish engulfing are now trapped long
- They will SELL when price returns to their entry level to exit with minimal loss
- The level that was support (1.0850 area) is now resistance — trapped bulls create supply on any return

**Pattern Failure Level creation:**
- When a bullish pattern is invalidated: the pattern's KEY HIGH becomes a FAILURE RESISTANCE level (new bearish reference)
- When a bearish pattern is invalidated: the pattern's KEY LOW becomes a FAILURE SUPPORT level (new bullish reference)
- Weight of the failure level: 60% of the original pattern's confirmed weight
- State: starts as Active (same lifecycle as other S/R levels)

**Implementation:**
```cpp
if(pattern.state == INVALIDATED) {
   SRLevel failure_level;
   if(pattern.direction == BULLISH)
      failure_level.price = pattern.key_high;
      failure_level.direction = BEARISH;
   else
      failure_level.price = pattern.key_low;
      failure_level.direction = BULLISH;
   failure_level.weight = pattern.confirmed_weight * 0.60;
   AddToPool(failure_level);
}
```

**Why this creates value:** Failed patterns are among the most reliable S/R levels in TA — everyone who traded the setup is trapped, and their exit orders create highly predictable supply/demand at the failure level. By tracking and adding these to the pool, the EA captures institutional "trapped trader" levels.

### ═══ EXTENSION 11: Pattern Sequence / Series (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** When MULTIPLE consecutive or proximate candle patterns of the same or related types form in the same price zone, they create a "pattern cluster zone" with significantly higher weight than any single pattern.

**Consecutive Doji Series (Doji Cluster):**
- 2 consecutive Dojis: "Twin Doji" — moderate indecision reinforcement
- 3 consecutive Dojis: "Triple Doji" — extreme indecision, anticipate a major directional break. Weight boost: +0.5
- Combined zone: the range of all Dojis = the decision zone. Whichever boundary breaks first = the direction for the next major move.

**Consecutive Hammer Formation:**
- Multiple Hammers on consecutive bars in the same price area = "Hammer Zone"
- Each successive Hammer hitting the same low = reinforcing SSL / equal lows (adds to E1/E2 pool depth from SMC)
- Weight boost per additional Hammer: +0.25 (up to +0.75 max)

**Alternating Bull/Bear Engulfing (Indecision Zone):**
- Bull Engulfing then Bear Engulfing then Bull Engulfing = major battle zone
- The HIGH and LOW of the entire alternating cluster = the boundaries of an indecision/accumulation zone
- Weight boost: +0.3 (high uncertainty = high future reactivity at zone boundaries)

**Escalating Wick Series (Ladder buildup):**
- Three candles with progressively longer wicks in the same direction (pattern 49/50 Ladder)
- Already captured as Ladder Bottom/Top in the pattern type, but the individual wick-candles before the final reversal also create sub-levels
- Each wick-candle's wick extreme = a sub-support level within the larger ladder pattern zone

**Implementation:**

### ═══ EXTENSION 12: Gap-Enhanced Patterns (Attribute) ═══

**What it is:** Some candle patterns are dramatically more powerful when they occur WITH PRICE GAPS — the gap itself provides additional evidence of institutional urgency and creates a separate FVG-type level.

**Gap-enhanced pattern variants:**

**Gapped Hammer:**
- Hammer that opens WITH A GAP DOWN from the previous candle's close
- The gap + Hammer combination = price gapped to a new low (sellers overwhelmed in pre-market) AND then reversed (buyers absorbed everything and more)
- This creates: (1) Hammer low level, (2) the gap zone as a bullish FVG-type level
- Weight boost: +0.3 (gap confirms urgency of the reversal)

**Gapped Engulfing:**
- Bullish Engulfing where Candle 2 OPENS WITH A GAP DOWN from Candle 1's close, then rallies to engulf
- The gap down = forced selling (stop-raid) before the engulf
- Equivalent to an AMD Manipulation Low + Engulfing = ultra-high significance
- Weight boost: +0.5

**Gapped Morning Star (= Abandoned Baby Bullish, Pattern 29):**
- Double gaps already create the Abandoned Baby pattern (already counted separately)
- Single-gap Morning Star = gap between Candle 1 and the Star, or between Star and Candle 3
- One-side gapped = +0.3 vs zero-gap Morning Star

**Gapped Kicker (= Standard Kicker Definition):**
- The Kicker patterns (31/32) are by definition gap-based (open-to-open gap). Their weight already reflects this.

**Gap creation as standalone FVG:**
When any pattern includes a visible price gap (Candle N+1 open ≠ Candle N close by > 5 ticks):
- That gap = a separate FVG-type S/R level ADDED to the pool independently
- The candle pattern level AND the gap FVG level both exist simultaneously at nearby prices
- Their proximity = automatic 2-method WM_CONF_BONUS (+0.5) at that price zone

**Implementation:**
`_DetectGapInPattern(pattern_candles[])` → returns `has_gap`, `gap_size_pips`, `gap_direction` → applies gap_bonus and creates auxiliary FVG level if `has_gap = true` and `gap_size > MIN_GAP_THRESHOLD`.
