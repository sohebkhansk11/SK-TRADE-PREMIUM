### RSI Slope

**Definition:** `RSI_Slope = (RSI_current − RSI_N_bars_ago) / N` — the rate of change of RSI per bar over a rolling N-bar window (default N = 3).

**What it adds:** Slope quantifies the SPEED at which RSI is approaching or leaving an extreme. A fast-moving RSI slope means momentum is accelerating — the next S/R level in the slope's direction is being approached with increasing force. A decelerating slope means momentum is running out even before the oscillator extreme is reached.

**Weight modification by slope magnitude:**

| RSI Slope per bar | Context | S/R Weight Modification |
|---|---|---|
| > +8 (steep rising) | RSI racing toward overbought | Nearest resistance → +0.30 (momentum surging toward it) |
| +4 to +8 (rising) | Normal momentum build | Nearest resistance → +0.15 |
| −4 to +4 (flat) | Momentum neutral | No modification |
| −8 to −4 (falling) | Normal momentum fade | Nearest support → +0.15 |
| < −8 (steep falling) | RSI racing toward oversold | Nearest support → +0.30 |

**Slope deceleration signal:** If RSI Slope was > +5 two bars ago, > +3 one bar ago, and is now < +1 — RSI is decelerating while still approaching overbought. The resistance S/R level price is approaching gets an ADDITIONAL +0.20 (deceleration near resistance = momentum exhaustion confirmation = the level is much more likely to hold).

### RSI Momentum Direction

**Definition:** Whether RSI is currently rising, falling, or reversing direction, tracked across its internal 3-bar moving average: `RSI_Momentum = RSI_current vs SMA(RSI, 3)`.

**What it adds:** Direction tells you WHERE momentum is pointed relative to the S/R landscape — critical for knowing whether price approaching a level has momentum TOWARD it (higher probability of breakthrough) or is approaching with fading momentum (higher probability of reversal).

**Direction states and their S/R weight effects:**

| RSI Momentum Direction | Effect on approaching S/R |
|---|---|
| Rising and above 50: Bullish momentum active and building | Support below → +0.10 (won't need it). Resistance above → −0.10 (may be broken by momentum). If RSI also > 65 and rising → flip sign: resistance above → +0.25 (approaching overbought resistance WITH rising momentum = high reversal probability) |
| Rising and below 50: Recovering from oversold toward neutral | Support previously tagged → weight confirmed +0.15 (the support held and RSI is recovering from it). |
| Turning Down from above 70: RSI reversal confirmed | The resistance price level where RSI peaked → +0.20 permanent bonus (momentum turned at this price = resistance proven). |
| Turning Up from below 30: RSI reversal confirmed | Support level where RSI troughed → +0.20 permanent bonus (support absorbed selling). |
| Falling and below 50: Bearish momentum | Mirror of rising-above-50. |
| Flat (RSI same as 3 bars ago ± 1 point): Momentum dead | ALL nearby S/R levels → +0.10 (flat RSI momentum in either overbought or oversold = maximum reversal probability from those extremes). |

### RSI Projection Curve

**Definition:** Using the current RSI Slope, project the RSI value N bars into the future: `RSI_Projected(N) = RSI_current + RSI_Slope × N`. From this, calculate the price that corresponds to RSI reaching the overbought threshold (70), oversold threshold (30), and midpoint (50) at the current momentum trajectory.

**Price projection formula:** Given RSI_Slope and the current relationship between price change and RSI change (derived from the RSI formula's gain/loss ratio), estimate the price at which RSI will reach each threshold.

**What it creates:**

| Projection Level | Definition | S/R Role |
|---|---|---|
| **RSI OB Projection Price** | The price at which RSI is projected to reach 70 given current slope — the "where momentum exhausts" level | Anticipatory resistance. Weight = 0.70× of a normal RSI-confirmed resistance (projected, not yet confirmed). Becomes 1.0× weight once RSI actually reaches 70 at that price. |
| **RSI OS Projection Price** | Price at which RSI is projected to reach 30 | Anticipatory support. Same 0.70× anticipatory weight. |
| **RSI 50-Cross Projection Price** | Price at which RSI is projected to cross 50 (momentum neutral/flip point) | Equilibrium projection level — where the current momentum move runs out of excess and normalizes. Acts as a mid-target level. Weight = 0.50× (weaker than the OB/OS thresholds). |

**Projection curve behavior:** The RSI Projection Curve is a DYNAMIC level — it updates every bar as the slope changes. It is flagged in the S/R pool with a distinct marker (`RSI_PROJ` type flag) so the weight matrix knows it is anticipatory, not confirmed. When price reaches the projected level AND RSI simultaneously reaches 70/30/50 → the `RSI_PROJ` flag is replaced by a standard RSI extreme confirmation → weight upgrades to full confirmed weight.

**Integration with Slope and Momentum Direction:** RSI Slope + Direction + Projection Curve form a three-layer RSI intelligence system:
- Slope tells you HOW FAST RSI is moving
- Momentum Direction tells you WHICH WAY it is pointed
- Projection Curve tells you WHERE it will arrive and at what price

Together these three make the RSI weight modification system fully predictive rather than reactive — the S/R pool can be weight-adjusted BEFORE RSI reaches an extreme, not only after.
