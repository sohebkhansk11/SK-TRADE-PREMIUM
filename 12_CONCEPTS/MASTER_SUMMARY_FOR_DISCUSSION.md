  can recognise. At any instant only a handful are active near price. The number measures
  *expressive resolution*, not screen clutter.

## 3. THE COUNT-vs-ATTRIBUTE SPLIT (critical to understanding 84,309)

Every concept's "12 AI extensions" divide into two kinds. **This is the single most important
modelling decision in the whole framework** — get it wrong and the count either explodes or collapses.

- **COUNT-CONTRIBUTING dimensions** (multiply the type count):
  `type × grade × timeframe × state`. These are the ONLY four axes that ever multiply.
  Adding a new pattern type, a new grade, a new TF, or a new state changes the headline number.
- **ATTRIBUTE / CONTEXTUAL dimensions** (modify WEIGHT only, add ZERO rows):
  multi-TF alignment, Fib/round-number confluence, kill-zone timing, IPDA, displacement quality
  (DQS), freshness decay, liquidity-pool depth, volume signature, divergence, etc.
  These shift a level's tier up or down but never create a new countable class.

> Typical concept = **4 count-contributing extensions + 8 attribute extensions = 12 total.**
> So "12 advanced AI extensions" per concept is consistent across #9–#12, but only 4 of them
> touch the count. The other 8 are pure weight intelligence.

## 5. THE WEIGHT MATRIX — HOW ALL 12 CONVERGE INTO ONE NUMBER

All 84,309 possible level types feed a single pooled zone engine. When multiple levels cluster
within an ATR-scaled radius they merge into one **zone**, and the zone's weight is:

```
zone.weight = MAX(level_weights_in_cluster)
            + WM_CONF_BONUS[distinct_methodology_count]   // {0, 0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5}
            + 0.3 (if a round number is within proximity)
zone.weight = min(zone.weight, 10.0)
zone.tier   = floor( max(1.0, zone.weight) )              // Tier 1 … Tier 10
```

- `WM_CONF_BONUS` rewards **distinct METHODOLOGY agreement** (e.g. EMA + Swing + Pivot = 3 methods → +1.0).
  This is the cross-concept confluence engine — the reason 12 separate concepts are worth more
  together than apart.
- Concept #12 additionally has an **internal** convergence bonus (BB Upper + VWAP +2σ + Kumo Top
  all at one price) that stacks on top of the cross-methodology bonus.
- Final tier (1–10) is what the EA's scorer/gates consume.

## 6. THE TOUCH-DEPLETION ARC (Pattern B — Swings #4 & Trendlines #5)

S/R levels are not static — a level's strength rises then falls with how many times it's been tested.
Peak strength is at the **3rd touch**, then it depletes.

| Touch # | Tier | SwingMult | TouchBonus | Meaning |
|---------|------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | 5 | 0.65 | 0.00 | fresh, unproven |
| 2 | 8 | 0.88 | +0.50 | confirmed |
| 3 | **PEAK** | 0.99 | +1.00 | maximally proven |
| 4 | declining | 0.99 | +0.60 | weakening |
| 5 | declining | 0.97 | +0.20 | weak |
| 6+ | depleted | 0.97 | −0.30 | likely to break |

> Logic: each test consumes resting liquidity. By the 6th touch the level has been "eaten" and is
> a break candidate, not a bounce candidate. This arc is what makes the EA respect a 3-touch level
> but fade a 7-touch one.

**Layer 2 — 7 pure oscillators (all attribute, no price coordinate):**
RSI, Stochastic, MACD, CCI, Williams %R, ADX, MFI. They modify the weight of the nearest S/R level
when they hit extremes or diverge. **ADX is unique — the only oscillator that can DECREASE weight**
(strong trend → counter-trend levels get blown through, so their weight is cut).

**RSI 3-layer predictive sub-system (inside Extension 5):**
- **RSI Slope** — velocity of RSI (how fast momentum approaches an extreme). Deceleration near a
  level = exhaustion = +weight to that level.
- **RSI Momentum Direction** — turning points; a confirmed turn from >70 or <30 plants a
  **permanent +0.20** on the price level where RSI reversed.
- **RSI Projection Curve** — projects the future price at which RSI will hit 70/30/50, creating
  *anticipatory* S/R (0.70× weight until confirmed, then upgrades to full).
  Together: Slope = how fast, Direction = which way, Projection = where it arrives → fully
  **predictive** rather than reactive weighting.

**12 extensions = 4 count** (type expansion, grade, TF, state) **+ 8 attribute** (oscillator
confluence stack, BB squeeze/expansion/walk state, VWAP slope & reclaim, Volume-Profile HVN/LVN
overlay, cross-oscillator divergence engine, multi-indicator convergence at one price, ATR-normalised
mean-reversion score, Ichimoku cloud geometry [thickness/color/twist/flat-Kijun]).

These are the open design questions worth pressure-testing:

1. **Count integrity** — is the `type × grade × TF × state` multiply the right backbone, or does it
   over-count (e.g. is "Weak grade × M1 × Invalidated" ever a real, useful row)? Should some
   states be attribute, not count?
2. **The attribute weight budget** — many extensions add +0.1…+0.9. With ~8 attribute layers per
   concept and cross-concept confluence on top, what stops weights from saturating at the 10.0 cap
   constantly? Is the `MAX(level_weights) + bonus` merge rule the right ceiling discipline?
3. **TF explosion** — every concept multiplies ×15 TFs, but the default gates disable most sub-M15.
   Is tracking 15 TFs of count justified when ~10 are gated off by default?
4. **Depletion arc vs state machine** — Swings/Trendlines use the touch-arc; SMC/Candle/Chart use a
   Fresh→…→Invalidated state machine. Should these be unified?
5. **The pattern-failure / trapped-trader levels** (#10, #11) and **VWAP reclaim** (#12) are some of
   the highest-conviction levels but are attribute-only. Should "failed pattern" be a first-class
   countable type?
6. **Cross-concept confluence (`WM_CONF_BONUS`)** rewards *distinct methodology* agreement — is the
   {0,0,0.5,1.0,1.5} curve aggressive enough, given confluence is the system's whole thesis?
7. **Implementation realism in pure MQL5** — VWAP, Volume Profile, Ichimoku, harmonic detection, and
   48 chart-pattern detectors are heavy. Which concepts give the most edge-per-CPU-cycle on an MT5
   single-thread OnTick budget?
8. **Foundation vs Institutional balance** — 52% of the taxonomy is the Institutional layer (#9–#12).
   Is that where the real predictive edge is, or is it complexity that dilutes the clean Foundation
   signals (#1–#5)?
